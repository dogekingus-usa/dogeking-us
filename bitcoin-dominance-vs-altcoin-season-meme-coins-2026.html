What Is Bitcoin Dominance (BTC.D)?

Bitcoin Dominance is the ratio of Bitcoin's market cap to the total crypto market cap, expressed as a percentage:

BTC.D = Bitcoin Market Cap ÷ Total Crypto Market Cap × 100

Example (June 2, 2026):
BTC Market Cap: $1.36 Trillion
Total Market Cap: $2.42 Trillion
BTC.D = 55.98%

BTC.D tells you where capital is flowing at the macro level. It's not a trading indicator in the traditional sense ” it's a sentiment and capital-flow barometer for the entire crypto asset class.

How to Read BTC.D Divergences: The Secret Sauce

The most powerful BTC.D signal isn't the level itself ” it's the divergence between BTC price action and BTC.D movement.

Bearish Divergence (Altcoin Warning)

BTC price rising + BTC.D falling = Capital leaving BTC even as price goes up. This sounds bullish, but it actually means the rally is fragile. Smart money is selling BTC into strength and buying alts. When BTC eventually drops, alts drop HARDER because they've already been bid up.

Bullish Divergence (Altcoin Opportunity)

BTC price falling + BTC.D also falling = Capital is fleeing crypto entirely OR rotating into alts. If total market cap stays flat or declines slowly while BTC.D falls, it means money is rotating INTO altcoins even during a BTC selloff. This is the most bullish altcoin signal.

Caveats & Warnings

⚠ BTC.D is NOT a standalone signal.

• BTC.D can be misleading during stablecoin dominance shifts. If stablecoin cap rises while BTC.D falls, capital isn't going to altcoins ” it's going to cash.
• BTC.D can stay elevated during crypto winter (2022•œ2023: BTC.D rose from 38% to 52% during bear market).
• New altcoins launch constantly. The total crypto market cap expands, which can suppress BTC.D even without rotation.
• Always combine BTC.D with total market cap and volume for a complete picture.
