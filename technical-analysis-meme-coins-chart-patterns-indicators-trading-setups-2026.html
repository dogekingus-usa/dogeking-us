    How to Draw Meme Coin Trend Lines That Hold

    The key insight from 2026 meme coin data is that trend lines work best when drawn on logarithmic rather than linear scales. Meme coins that go from $0.000001 to $0.0001 (a 100x move) appear as a vertical line on a linear chart, making trend lines impossible to draw meaningfully.     Rules for meme coin trend lines that work:

    • At least three touches: A trend line needs a minimum of three touches (not including the starting point) to be valid. Two-touch lines are coincidence.
    • Use the wicks, not the bodies: In meme coins, the wicks represent the true extremes of supply and demand. A trend line connecting wicks is more valid than one connecting candle bodies, because the wicks show where buyers and sellers actually stepped in.
    • 45-degree angle rule: Trends with a slope significantly steeper than 45 degrees (on log scale) are unsustainable. A meme coin trend at 60+ degrees will fall apart within 24 hours. A trend at 30-45 degrees can persist for weeks.
    • Volume confirms trend lines: A rising trend line with declining volume is a warning sign. The trend is being sustained by fewer and fewer participants. A single volume spike can break it.
    • Channel drawing: Parallel channels (connecting both swing highs and swing lows) are more reliable than single trend lines. When a meme coin is trading in a clean channel, the upper channel line is your sell zone and the lower channel line is your buy zone.
