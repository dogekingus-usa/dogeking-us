What to Do When Crypto Crashes — Don't Panic, Do This Instead

📅 June 5, 2026📈 Action Plan✅ Survival Guide⏱ 7 min read

You opened your portfolio and your stomach dropped. Bitcoin at $63K. Solana at $69. Your portfolio down 40-60%. The urge to sell everything and walk away is real.

Don't.

Panic selling during a crash is the single most expensive mistake in crypto investing. The data is clear: investors who sell during crashes lock in losses that never recover. Those who hold — and act strategically — recover every time.

Here's exactly what to do right now, step by step.

Step 1: Stop — Literally Stop What You're Doing

The first thing to do in a crash is nothing. Close your portfolio tracker. Close your exchange tabs. Take 30 minutes away from the screen.

Why? Because every decision you make while your heart is racing and your portfolio is flashing red will be the wrong one. The cortisol spike from watching your portfolio drop impairs decision-making. Studies of retail investors show that the worst trades happen during the first hour of a crash.

Walk away. Breathe. Come back with a clear head.

Step 2: Audit Your Portfolio — Not Your P&L

When you come back, do not look at how much money you've lost. That number will make you emotional. Instead, evaluate each asset on its fundamentals:

  • Is this a project I believed in at the high? If yes, the fundamentals haven't changed just because the price dropped.
  • Does this project have real users, revenue, and development activity? Solana is still processing 2,500+ TPS. Ethereum has $40B+ in DeFi TVL. Those fundamentals don't disappear because of macro headwinds.
  • Is the team still building? Check project socials and GitHub. If development is active during the crash, that's a strong signal.
  • Run a stability checklist: Is your crypto secured in a reliable wallet or trusted exchange with strong liquidity? Weak exchanges fail during crashes — it happened in 2022 with FTX and it will happen again.

Assets that pass this audit are holds or even buys. Assets that don't — projects with no development, dead communities, or questionable tokenomics — should be evaluated for sale regardless of market conditions.

Step 3: Secure Your Assets

Crashes expose weak infrastructure. Exchanges get overwhelmed. Wallets see traffic spikes. Withdrawals slow down. Secure your assets immediately:

  • Spread across trusted exchanges: Don't keep everything on one platform. Use MEXC, Binance, and OKX for redundancy.
  • Move long-term holds to cold storage: If you're not trading, your BTC/ETH/SOL belong in a hardware wallet.
  • Enable 2FA everywhere: If you haven't already, do it now. Crashes are when hackers target distracted investors.
  • Check withdrawal addresses: One wrong character and your funds are gone. Verify, then verify again.

Step 4: Assess Your Personal Situation

Before making any trading decisions, ask these questions honestly:

  • Do you need this money in the next 12 months? If yes, consider selling enough to secure your position. Financial stability comes before crypto profits.
  • Can you handle another 30-50% drop? If the thought makes you sick, reduce your position size to a level where you can sleep at night.
  • Is your emergency fund intact? If not, that's priority #1 — above any crypto decision.

There is no shame in selling to preserve your financial health. The goal is to make rational decisions, not heroic ones.

Step 5: Consider Buying — But With Strategy

If your personal situation is secure and your long-term conviction is strong, crashes are historically the best time to buy. But do it with a system, not impulse:

  • Use dollar-cost averaging — fixed amounts at regular intervals
  • Set limit orders at key support levels — don't buy at market
  • Keep dry powder — never deploy more than 30% of your total budget at once

Step 6: Set Alerts — Not Price Checks

The worst thing you can do is check prices every 30 minutes. It's emotional, exhausting, and leads to bad decisions.

Instead, set price alerts on your exchange for the levels that actually matter — your re-entry points, your stop-losses, and your take-profit targets. When the alert fires, open the app. Execute. Close it. Professional traders spend about 5-10 minutes per day on their portfolio during bear markets. Everyone else spends hours watching red candles and accomplishing nothing.

Step 7: Make a Plan for the Recovery

Crypto crashes don't last forever. Every previous crash was followed by a recovery and a new all-time high. The investors who profit most aren't the ones who panic — they're the ones who have a plan for what to do when the market turns.

  • Set take-profit targets: Decide now what price you'll sell 25%, 50%, and 75% of your position.
  • Plan your exit ladder: Selling all at once is as risky as buying all at once. Plan a staggered exit.
  • Write it down: A plan in your head is a wish. A plan on paper is a strategy.
