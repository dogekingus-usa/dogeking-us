The Beginner's Guide to Solana Staking: Earn Passive Income with SOL in 2026

June 3, 20266 min read

Staking Solana (SOL) is the easiest way to earn passive income in crypto without trading. With current APY rates of 6-8%, staking SOL generates steady returns while you hold. Here's exactly how to do it in 2026.

What Is Solana Staking?

Staking means locking up your SOL to help secure the network. In return, you receive staking rewards ” paid in SOL. Unlike Ethereum, Solana doesn't have a minimum staking amount. You can stake as little as 0.01 SOL.

Step 1: Choose a Wallet

Phantom is the easiest wallet for staking. It has a built-in staking interface. Open Phantom, click "Stake" in the menu, and you'll see the staking dashboard. Solflare also offers native staking with more detailed validator analytics.

Step 2: Choose a Validator

Validators are the entities that process transactions on Solana. When you stake, you delegate your SOL to a validator who earns rewards on your behalf. Key factors when choosing:

  • Commission: The percentage of rewards the validator takes. Most charge 5-10%. Lower is better.
  • Uptime: Validators with 99%+ uptime earn the most rewards. Avoid validators below 95%.
  • Vote credits: A measure of validator reliability. Higher is better.
Recommended validators for beginners: Solana Foundation (0% commission), Cogent Crypto (5%), Laine (6%).

Step 3: Delegate SOL

  1. Open Phantom wallet
  2. Click "Stake" on the sidebar
  3. Enter the amount of SOL to stake
  4. Select a validator from the list
  5. Confirm the transaction (costs ~$0.0002 in fees)

That's it. Your SOL is now earning rewards. Rewards accrue every epoch (2-3 days) and are automatically added to your staked balance.

Unstaking: How to Get SOL Back

Unstaking SOL takes 2-3 epochs (~4-6 days). During this period, your SOL is not earning rewards. To unstake:

  1. Go to Phantom —™ Stake
  2. Click your active stake account
  3. Click "Unstake"
  4. Confirm the transaction
  5. Wait 4-6 days for SOL to be available in your wallet

Risks

  • Slashing: If a validator acts maliciously, a portion of staked funds can be slashed. Very rare on Solana ” mostly theoretical.
  • Lock-up period: The 4-6 day unstaking period means you can't sell immediately in a crash.
  • Validator risk: A poorly performing validator earns fewer rewards. Choose wisely.
