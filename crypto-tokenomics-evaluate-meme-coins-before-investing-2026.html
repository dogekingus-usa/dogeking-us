The most sustainable meme coin projects use one of three inflation models:

  • Fixed Supply (Disinflationary): All tokens are minted at launch. No new tokens are created. Example: DogeKing (DKING) with its fixed 1 billion supply.
  • Halving-Based (Decelerating Inflation): New issuance decreases on a fixed schedule. Block rewards or staking yields halve periodically, reducing inflation over time.
  • Bonding-Curve Adaptive: Inflation adjusts based on protocol revenue, staking participation, or automated market making parameters. Rare in pure meme coins but increasingly common in "meme-fi" hybrids.

Critical Alert: If a meme coin's whitepaper does not disclose its inflation schedule, treat this as an intentional omission. Transparent projects publish their full token emission curve. Projects hiding inflation are almost always planning to dilute early holders.

Distribution analysis tools have matured significantly. Bubble Maps visualizes wallet clusters, showing connections between addresses. Birdeye and DexScreener show top holder percentages directly on their token pages.

Here is the distribution checklist you should run before every trade:

  • Top 10 Holders: Should hold less than 20% of total supply combined. If they hold more than 50%, the token is controlled by a small group.
  • Top 100 Holders: Should hold less than 60% of total supply. Anything above 80% means near-total centralization.
  • Team Wallets: Can you identify which wallets belong to the team? Are they labeled on Solscan or Etherscan? Unlabeled team tokens are a major red flag.
  • Presale Wallets: How many unique addresses participated in the presale? Fewer than 500 participants for a hyped launch suggests Sybil attacks or insider concentration.
  • Exchange Wallets: Has the token been listed on any CEX? Large holdings on exchange deposit wallets suggest imminent selling pressure.
