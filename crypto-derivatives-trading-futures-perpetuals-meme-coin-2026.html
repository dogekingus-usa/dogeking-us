What Are Crypto Derivatives?

Crypto derivatives are financial contracts whose value derives from an underlying cryptocurrency. Instead of buying the actual token, you're trading a contract that tracks its price.

Perpetual Futures Explained

Perpetual futures (perps) are the most popular derivative product in crypto. Unlike traditional futures, they never expire. Instead, a funding rate mechanism keeps the perp price anchored to the spot price.

How Funding Rates Work:

  • If more traders are long (betting price goes up), the funding rate is positive.
  • Longs pay shorts every funding interval (typically 1 hour on Solana).
  • If funding is negative, shorts pay longs.
  • During extreme bullish sentiment, funding can spike to 0.1-0.5% per hour â€” that's 2-12% per day just to hold a position.

Funding Rate Trap: Many new traders ignore funding rates and get liquidated by negative carry. A position that's fundamentally correct can still lose money if funding rates are unfavorable. Always check current funding rates before opening a position.

Essential Risk Rules:

  1. Never use more than 2-5x leverage on meme coin pairs. Meme coins already have 20-50% daily swings. Adding leverage is a recipe for liquidation.
  2. Always set a stop-loss. On Drift and Zeta, you can set stop-loss orders that execute at a specific price. This is non-negotiable.
  3. Keep your liquidation price far away. Always maintain at least 30% margin buffer. If your liquidation price is within 10% of the current price, you're overleveraged.
  4. Size positions as a percentage of your total portfolio. Never risk more than 2% of your derivatives account on a single trade.
  5. Track funding costs. High funding rates can silently drain your account. Factor funding into your profit calculations.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overleveraging: 10x leverage on a meme coin perp means a 10% price move liquidates you. Meme coins frequently move 20-50% in a single day.
  • Ignoring funding rates: A $10,000 position at 0.1% hourly funding costs $24/day. Over a month, that's $720 in funding costs alone.
  • Adding to losing positions: Averaging down in a leveraged position is dangerous. A small additional move against you can trigger liquidation.
  • Trading without stop-losses: In crypto derivatives, a 50% move can happen in minutes. Without stop-losses, you can go from profitable to liquidated before you can react.
  • Not understanding liquidation mechanics: Different platforms calculate liquidation prices differently. Understand how your platform handles liquidation before trading.
