Liquidity Pools on Solana: How to Provide Liquidity and Earn Fees on DEXs in 2026

Published: May 25, 2026 | Category: DeFi | Reading Time: 9 min

Liquidity pools are the engine that powers every decentralized exchange on Solana. Without liquidity providers (LPs), DEXs like Raydium, Orca, and Jupiter would have zero trading volume. By depositing your tokens into a liquidity pool, you earn a share of the trading fees generated by the exchange — typically 0.01-0.30% per trade.

This guide covers everything you need to know about providing liquidity on Solana in 2026, including how it works, which platforms offer the best returns, and how to manage the risks.

How Liquidity Pools Work

A liquidity pool is a smart contract that holds two or more tokens. LPs deposit equal values of both tokens (e.g., 50% SOL + 50% USDC). Traders swap against this pool, paying a fee. The fee is distributed proportionally to all LPs based on their share of the pool.

Solana uses an Automated Market Maker (AMM) model. The constant product formula (x * y = k) automatically sets prices based on the ratio of tokens in the pool. When someone buys SOL from a SOL/USDC pool, the pool has less SOL and more USDC, so the price of SOL increases.

1. Raydium — Solana's Original AMM

Raydium is the most established DEX on Solana with the deepest liquidity and highest trading volume. It offers both standard AMM pools and CPMM (Constant Product Market Maker) pools for volatile pairs.

  • Standard pools: Deposit two tokens at 50/50 ratio. Earn fees from trades.
  • Staked LP tokens: Raydium lets you stake your LP tokens in "Farms" to earn additional RAY token rewards on top of trading fees.
  • Top pools: SOL/USDC (0.01% fee, $50M+ TVL), SOL/USDT, SOL/mSOL

2. Orca — Best for Concentrated Liquidity

Orca introduced concentrated liquidity to Solana, allowing LPs to concentrate their capital within a specific price range for higher capital efficiency. A standard pool earning 10% APY at full range might earn 30-50% APY when concentrated in a tight range.

  • Whirlpools: Concentrated liquidity pools with customizable price ranges
  • Non-custodial: Full control of your position at all times
  • Best for: Stablecoin pairs (USDC/USDT) where you can concentrate in a narrow range

3. Meteora — Dynamic Liquidity Pools

Meteora offers Dynamic Liquidity Market Maker (DLMM) pools that automatically adjust concentration based on trading activity. This is the most advanced LP option on Solana.

  • Dynamic bins: Liquidity is automatically concentrated around the current price
  • Auto-compounding: Fees are automatically reinvested into your position
  • Best for: Active LPs who want optimal capital efficiency without manual adjustments

Impermanent Loss Explained

Impermanent loss (IL) is the biggest risk for liquidity providers. It occurs when the price ratio of the two tokens in the pool changes after you deposit. The more the prices diverge, the larger the IL.

Real Example: You deposit $1,000 in a SOL/USDC pool (500 SOL + 500 USDC) when SOL is at $100. SOL price doubles to $200. Arbitrageurs buy your SOL at the lower pool price. When you withdraw, you have less SOL and more USDC than you deposited. Your total value is now ~$1,414 — a 41% gain vs 100% if you just held SOL. The IL cost you 59% relative to holding.

LP Fee Calculations

Your earnings as an LP depend on:

  • Trading volume: More volume = more fees. SOL/USDC generates the most fees of any Solana pool.
  • Your share of the pool: If you own 0.1% of a pool, you earn 0.1% of all fees.
  • Fee tier: Higher fee pools (0.25%) earn more per trade but attract less volume.
  • Reward tokens: Many pools offer bonus tokens (RAY, ORCA, KMNO) on top of trading fees.

Typical LP APYs on Solana in 2026: Stablecoin pairs (5-12% APY), SOL pairs (10-25% APY), meme coin pairs (20-80% APY, higher risk).

Yield Farming vs LP: What's the Difference?

  • Liquidity Providing: Deposit tokens into a pool, earn trading fees. Lower risk, steady returns.
  • Yield Farming (Staked LP): Deposit LP tokens into a "Farm" to earn additional governance tokens. Higher risk, higher potential returns.
  • Lending: Deposit tokens into a lending protocol (Kamino, Marginfi). Earn interest from borrowers. Lowest risk.

How to Start Providing Liquidity

  1. Choose a platform (Raydium recommended for beginners)
  2. Select a pool (SOL/USDC is the safest starting point)
  3. Ensure you have equal value of both tokens
  4. Approve both tokens and confirm the deposit transaction
  5. Receive LP tokens representing your share of the pool
  6. Monitor your position's value and fee earnings regularly

Risks to Consider

  • Impermanent loss: The primary risk. Choose stable pairs (USDC/USDT) to minimize IL.
  • Smart contract risk: DEXs can be hacked. Raydium and Orca have been audited multiple times.
  • Meme coin volatility: Pools with meme coins have extreme IL risk. Only LP with tokens you're willing to hold long-term.
  • Slippage on withdrawal: Large withdrawals from small pools can suffer price impact.

⚠️ Golden Rule: Never provide liquidity with tokens you're not willing to hold indefinitely. If a token's price dumps 90%, you'll still be in the pool with your remaining tokens. LP when you're bullish on both assets in the pair.
