DogeKing ($DKING) has established itself as one of the most talked-about meme coins on the Solana blockchain in 2026. But beyond the crown emojis and community hype, there's a carefully designed tokenomics model that governs how the token behaves. In this guide, we break down everything you need to know about DogeKing's token supply, the 1% burn mechanism, allocation across different pools, and how the tokenomics compare to other popular Solana meme coins.

1. Tokenomics Overview

DogeKing was launched on the Solana blockchain with a fixed supply model designed to create long-term value through scarcity. Unlike inflationary tokens that continuously mint new supply, every $DKING transaction actively reduces the circulating supply through an automated burn mechanism.

📊 $DKING Tokenomics at a Glance

Total Supply: 1,000,000,000 $DKING (1 Billion)
Blockchain: Solana
Burn Rate: 1% per transaction
Contract Address: UnKPK9oomuhdJz7cZAbaBEB5p3NhNVb6oUwyVP4oGJR
Token Standard: SPL (Solana Program Library)

The fixed supply of 1 billion tokens was chosen to balance accessibility (low per-token price for new investors) with scarcity (limited total supply). As the burn mechanism removes tokens from circulation, the remaining supply becomes progressively scarcer over time.

3. The 1% Burn Mechanism

Every $DKING transaction — whether buying, selling, or transferring — incurs a 1% burn fee. This means 1% of every transaction's volume is permanently removed from circulation by sending tokens to a dead wallet address.

🔥 How the Burn Works

When you trade $DKING on a Solana DEX, 1% of the traded amount is automatically sent to a burn wallet (an address with no known private key). These tokens are gone forever — they cannot be recovered or re-minted. This creates a constant deflationary pressure on the token supply.

The math behind the burn: If $DKING sees $10 million in daily trading volume (common for established Solana meme coins), approximately $100,000 worth of tokens are burned daily. At $DKING's current price, that removes roughly 5-10 million tokens from circulation each month.

Over time, this burn mechanism creates increasing scarcity. As the circulating supply decreases, each remaining token represents a larger share of the total value — assuming demand remains constant or grows. This is similar to the deflationary mechanics used by tokens like Shiba Inu and other top meme coins.
