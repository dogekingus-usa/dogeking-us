What Are DAOs? The Core Concepts

A DAO is an organization whose rules are encoded as smart contracts on a blockchain. Instead of a CEO, board of directors, or management committee, DAOs rely on token holders who vote on proposals, and smart contracts that automatically execute approved decisions. No single person can override the protocol ¢â'¬âEUR the code enforces the collective will.

Every DAO is defined by three structural layers. The governance token determines voting weight ¢â'¬âEUR one token typically equals one vote, though quadratic and conviction-based variants exist. The proposal framework defines how community members submit ideas, how they reach a quorum (minimum participation threshold), and how votes are tallied. The treasury smart contract holds the DAO's assets and only releases funds when an approved proposal's execution conditions are met.

DAO Voting Mechanisms: How Decisions Get Made

The voting mechanism a DAO uses determines who has power, how quickly decisions move, and whether minority stakeholders have meaningful influence. Understanding these mechanisms is essential for anyone participating in Solana DAO governance.

Token-Weighted Voting (One Token, One Vote)

The simplest and most common model. Each governance token equals one vote. Whales holding large token supplies dominate decision-making. Approximately 60% of Solana DAOs use pure token-weighted voting. Pros: simple, transparent, aligned with economic stake. Cons: plutocratic ¢â'¬âEUR large holders control outcomes; low-vote-weight participants have minimal influence.

Quadratic Voting

Voting power scales with the square root of tokens held ¢â'¬âEUR if Alice holds 100 tokens and Bob holds 10,000 tokens, Alice's voting weight is 10 and Bob's is 100 (not 10,000). This dramatically reduces whale dominance and amplifies the voice of smaller holders. Conviction Voting

Conviction voting is particularly well-suited for ongoing resource allocation decisions like grant programs and treasury diversification strategies.

Treasury Execution Models

Not all DAO treasury actions require full community votes. Solana DAOs use three tiers of treasury control. Small disbursements (under 10,000 USDC equivalent) are handled by automated smart contracts or multi-sig teams with fast execution. Medium proposals (10,000-100,000 USDC) require a standard governance vote with 48-hour voting periods. Large proposals (over 100,000 USDC) require extended voting periods (72-168 hours), time-locked execution (delay of 24-72 hours after approval), and often require a super-majority threshold (60% or 66% approval instead of simple majority).
