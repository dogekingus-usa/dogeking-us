What Accumulation Looks Like for Meme Coins

Key Indicators for Spotting Accumulation

2. RSI Divergence on Weekly Timeframes. If price is making lower lows while the weekly RSI (Relative Strength Index) is making higher lows, that's bullish divergence. Smart money is buying while price tells a different story.

3. Stablecoin Supply Ratio (SSR). When the SSR is high (meaning there's lots of stablecoin buying power relative to market cap), it signals that dry powder is building. This typically precedes accumulation. When whales are holding stablecoins rather than volatile assets, they're preparing to buy.

4. Exchange Outflow Spikes. When large amounts of meme coins move from exchanges to private wallets, that's accumulation behavior. Whales don't move tokens to cold storage during bull runs â€” they do it when they're collecting at low prices.

Distribution in meme coins is notoriously difficult to spot because the volatility creates noise that masks the underlying shift in supply-demand dynamics. But there are reliable tells:

Exchange Inflow Spikes: When large amounts of meme coins start flowing back to exchanges after a prolonged accumulation period, whales are preparing to sell. Track this data on Solscan or on-chain analytics tools.

Whale Wallet Behavior: During distribution, you'll see smart money wallets making large transfers to exchange wallets. They may also begin selling into buying pressure â€” placing large sell orders just above current price to absorb buying momentum.

How to Survive (and Profit From) Distribution

Look at the total meme coin market cap (available on CoinGecko). Is it above the 200-day moving average and rising? Follow the trend. Below it and declining? Get defensive. Use the MEMECOIN index or track the top 10 meme coins by market cap to gauge sector strength.
