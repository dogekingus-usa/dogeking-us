2. How Liquid Staking Works

The mechanics behind liquid staking are elegant but worth understanding:

  1. You deposit your native staking asset (e.g., 100 SOL) into a liquid staking protocol's smart contract.
  2. The protocol stakes your tokens across a diversified set of validators to maximize rewards and minimize slashing risk.
  3. You receive an equivalent amount of liquid staking tokens (e.g., ~100 mSOL or JitoSOL) in your wallet.
  4. Your LST appreciates in value relative to the native asset as staking rewards accumulate. Over time, 1 mSOL becomes worth more than 1 SOL.

    8. Risks and Considerations

    Liquid staking isn't risk-free. Here are the key risks to understand:

    • Smart Contract Risk: Liquid staking protocols are smart contracts that could have bugs or vulnerabilities. Marinade and Jito have both undergone multiple audits, but no code is 100% safe.
    • Slashing Risk: If validators in the protocol's set get slashed (e.g., for double-signing or going offline), LST holders bear a proportional loss. This is mitigated by validator diversification but not eliminated.
    • LST Depegging Risk: During extreme market volatility, LSTs can trade below their underlying value (depeg from the native asset). In May 2026, this is rare for major LSTs like mSOL and JitoSOL but can happen in black-swan events.
    • Protocol Governance Risk: Protocol parameters (fees, validator set, unstaking conditions) can change via governance votes, potentially affecting your returns or experience.
    • Liquidity Risk: While mSOL and JitoSOL have deep liquidity, smaller LSTs may have thin markets where slippage is significant.

    10. Frequently Asked Questions

    Is liquid staking safe compared to traditional staking?

    Liquid staking adds smart contract risk but reduces validator concentration risk through diversification. For reputable protocols like Marinade and Jito, the overall risk profile is comparable to traditional staking for most users.

    How is the yield on LSTs paid?

    Yield accrues through the appreciation of the LST relative to the native asset. If you deposit 100 SOL and receive 100 mSOL, over time your 100 mSOL becomes redeemable for 101, 102, 103+ SOL as rewards accumulate. You don't need to claim or compound manually.

    Can I lose money with liquid staking?

    Yes, if the LST depegs from SOL (trades below its redemption value) or if you use leverage and get liquidated. If you simply hold the LST without leverage, your principal in SOL terms grows over time, but its USD value depends on SOL's market price.

    What are the tax implications of liquid staking?

    In most jurisdictions, receiving LSTs in exchange for staked tokens is considered a taxable event (disposal of native asset for LST). The appreciation of LST value over time may also be taxable as income or capital gains. Consult a tax professional familiar with crypto.
