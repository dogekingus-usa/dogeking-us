1. The 2024 Halving Aftermath: Supply Squeeze Accelerates

Bitcoin's fourth halving occurred in April 2024, cutting block rewards from 6.25 BTC to 3.125 BTC per block. History shows that the 12-18 months following a halving are when the most explosive price action occurs:

  • 2012 Halving: BTC was $12 ” 12 months later: $1,000+ (8,000%+)
  • 2016 Halving: BTC was $650 ” 18 months later: $19,800 (3,000%+)
  • 2020 Halving: BTC was $8,600 ” 18 months later: $69,000 (700%+)
  • 2024 Halving: BTC was ~$63,000 ” 18 months later (mid-2025): ???

As of June 2026, we're approximately 26 months post-halving. Historically, Bitcoin peaks 12-18 months after the halving date. If history repeats, the cycle top should have occurred in late 2025. However, this cycle is different ” and that's what makes it uniquely bullish.

Why This Cycle Is Different:
• The 2024 halving reduced daily new supply from ~900 BTC to ~450 BTC
• Spot Bitcoin ETFs launched in January 2024 and now absorb 5-10x the daily new supply
• The "sell pressure" has been structurally reduced while "buy pressure" has structurally increased

• Macro Recession

• Regulatory Crackdown

While the 2024-2026 regulatory environment has been the most crypto-friendly in US history, a change in administration or a major exchange collapse could trigger panic selling.

• ETF Reversals

If geopolitical tensions cause a broad risk-off move, we could see sustained ETF outflows. GBTC-style discount arbitrage unwinding could also pressure prices.

• Miner Capitulation
