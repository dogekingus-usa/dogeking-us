What Is Meme Coin Sniping?

Meme coin sniping is the practice of buying a newly launched token as soon as it becomes tradable — often within the same block the liquidity pool opens. The goal is to acquire tokens at the absolute lowest possible price before the broader market discovers the launch.

On Solana, sniping happens in three primary environments:

  • Bonding Curve Launches (Pump.fun): Tokens launched via bonding curves where price increases as more tokens are bought. Snipers monitor for new curve activations.
  • Raydium Pool Launches: When a token graduates from Pump.fun or launches natively on Raydium, the first LP provision creates a trading opportunity.
  • Mint & Liquidity Add Events: New token mints paired with immediate liquidity adds on DEX aggregators.

How Sniping Bots Work on Solana

Solana's 400-millisecond block time makes it the fastest chain for sniping. Here's the technical flow:

  1. Monitoring: The bot monitors the Solana blockchain for new token mints, liquidity pool creation, or Pump.fun bonding curve activations.
  2. Filtering: Pre-configured filters screen for liquidity amount, LP burn status, mint authority revocation, and social links.
  3. Transaction Construction: When a qualifying launch is detected, the bot constructs a buy transaction with optimized priority fees to land in the next block.
  4. Submission: The transaction is submitted via private RPC endpoints (Triton, Helius) or via the Jito Block Engine for MEV protection.
  5. Execution: If successful, the bot holds or sells according to configured take-profit and stop-loss rules.
⚠ Warning: Poorly configured bots buy everything — including rugs, honey pots, and malicious tokens. Always pair your sniper with pre-sale filters and a due diligence checklist. Never run a sniper without safety guards.

Sniping on Pump.fun: The Launchpad Advantage

Pump.fun dominates meme coin launches on Solana, with thousands of tokens created daily. Sniping on Pump.fun is the most accessible entry point for new snipers.

How Pump.fun Launches Work

Tokens on Pump.fun follow a bonding curve: early buyers get lower prices, and the price rises as the curve fills. When the curve reaches ~$63K in market cap, the token graduates to Raydium with automatic liquidity. Snipers target the first few buys on the curve.

Pump.fun Sniping Setup

To snipe on Pump.fun effectively:

  • Use a dedicated sniping wallet (never your main holding wallet)

Raydium Pool Launch Sniping

When a Pump.fun token graduates or a project launches natively on Raydium, the liquidity pool creation event is the sniping trigger. Raydium sniping offers larger position sizes but requires more capital.

Types of Raydium Sniping

  • Graduation Sniping: Buying immediately when a Pump.fun token hits Raydium. The bot detects the pool creation and buys within the same block.
  • Concentrated Liquidity Pool Sniping: Raydium CLMM pools allow concentrated positions. Sniping these offers deeper liquidity for larger buys.
  • Permissionless Pool Sniping: Any token can create a pool. These require extra filtering — many are scams.
💡 Pro Tip: The biggest gains often come from tokens that don't graduate from Pump.fun but launch directly on Raydium with verified team wallets and locked liquidity. These fly under the radar of most snipers.

Manual Sniping: How to Snipe Without a Bot

You don't need a bot to snipe effectively. Manual sniping, done right, beats 90% of poorly configured bots. Here's how:

Step 1: Set Up Your Monitoring

Use DexScreener or Birdeye with filters set to "New Pairs" on Solana. Set minimum liquidity to $5,000 and minimum age to <1 minute. Watch the feed like a hawk.

Step 2: Pre-Configure Your DEX

Open Jupiter or Raydium in a browser tab with SOL pre-approved for swap. Set slippage to 15-25% (high for sniping, necessary for execution). Have your target token address ready to paste.

Step 3: Pre-Fund Your Wallet

Always keep 0.5-1 SOL in your trading wallet. Set priority fee to at least 0.01 SOL for fast inclusion.

Step 4: Execute in Under 10 Seconds

When you spot a promising launch: (1) Copy token address, (2) Paste into Jupiter, (3) Hit swap. Practice this sequence until it takes under 10 seconds.

Step 5: Have an Exit Plan

Set limit orders via Jupiter or Trojan for 2x-5x take profit. Use a hard stop-loss at -50% for early positions. Never diamond-hand a snipe — 90% of meme coins go to zero.

Risk Management: Avoiding Rugs & Honey Pots

Sniping is the riskiest form of meme coin trading. Here's a due diligence framework to filter out scams:

The 5-Second Safety Check

  1. Mint Revoked? If the mint authority is still active, the dev can print infinite tokens and dump on you. Skip if not revoked.
  2. LP Burned? Check if >80% of liquidity is burned. Unburned LP = rug risk. Burn check is non-negotiable.
  3. Honeypot Check? Can you sell? Use an RPC simulation or tools like Honeypot.is to verify both buy and sell work.
  4. Top Holder Concentration? If the top 10 wallets hold >20% of supply, one whale can crash the price. Proceed with caution.
  5. Socials Exist? A Telegram and Twitter with >24 hours of history suggest a real project. Fresh accounts (<1 hour) are red flags.
🛑 Hard Rule: Never snipe tokens that fail ALL five checks. Even three check failures should give you pause. The best snipers skip 95% of launches — they wait for the 5% that pass safety screens.

Advanced Sniping Strategies

Jito Bundling

Jito bundles allow you to submit multiple transactions atomically — buy, take profit, and transfer in a single bundle. This prevents front-running and sandwich attacks. Advanced snipers use Jito bundling to:

  • Buy a token and immediately set limit orders in the same bundle
  • Buy with multiple wallets simultaneously (distribution strategy)
  • Sell into liquidity spikes before bots can front-run

Multi-Wallet Sniping

Run 3-10 wallets with small capital each (0.1-0.5 SOL). When one wallet hits a 10x, the rest cover losses from dozens of failed snipes. This portfolio approach smooths returns.

Copy Sniping

Monitor known profitable sniping wallets (identifiable on-chain via Birdeye or Solscan). When they buy, you buy the same token in the next block. Not as fast as bot sniping, but piggybacks on their research.

Pre-Launch Detection

Monitor token mint events on Solana via Helius webhooks. Set alerts for mint transactions from known launchpad addresses (Pump.fun, Moonshot, MakeNow). This gives you a 1-2 second head start.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much money do I need to start sniping?

Start with 1-2 SOL ($150-300) minimum. This covers gas fees, priority fees across 20-50 trades, and allows for meaningful position sizes (0.05-0.1 SOL per snipe).

What's the best sniping bot for beginners?

Trojan offers the best balance of features and usability for beginners. It has built-in safety filters, Pump.fun integration, and a straightforward Telegram interface. Maestro is a close second with superior copy trading features.

How many snipes should I expect to lose?

Even pros lose 70-80% of snipes. The key is position sizing. Lose small (0.05 SOL per snipe), win big (1-2 SOL on a 20x). One 20x covers 20 losses. Focus on hit rate management, not avoiding losses entirely.

Is sniping legal?

Yes, sniping is legal. You're simply executing trades faster than other market participants. However, running MEV bots that front-run user transactions or execute sandwich attacks may violate exchange terms of service on some platforms.

Can I snipe from my phone?

Yes! Trojan, Maestro, and BonkBot all work via Telegram on mobile. Manual sniping is also possible with Phantom Mobile + DexScreener. Mobile sniping is slower (5-15 second delay) but works for catching mid-curve entries on Pump.fun.
