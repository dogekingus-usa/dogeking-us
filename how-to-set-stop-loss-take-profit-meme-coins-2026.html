Stop Loss vs Stop Limit vs Trailing Stop: Which Order Type to Use

Before we get into the how-to, let's clarify the three main order types and when to use each:

Order TypeHow It WorksBest For
Stop Loss (Market)Sells at the best available price once the stop price is hit. May experience slippage on volatile meme coins.Fast exits during crashes. Priority = getting out fast over getting the best price.
Stop LimitSells only at your specified limit price (or better) once the stop is triggered. Wont sell below your limit.Protecting profits in normal market conditions. Risk: order may not fill in a fast crash.
Trailing StopA dynamic stop loss that follows the price up. Locks in profits as price rises, but stays fixed if price falls.Capturing big runners. Let's winners run while protecting against reversals.

For meme coins: Use market stop losses for safety (slippage is the price of survival). Use trailing stops during pump phases to maximize gains.

How to Set Stop Loss Orders on Centralized Exchanges (Binance, Bybit, Kraken)

Centralized exchanges offer the most reliable stop loss functionality. Here's how to set them up on the three most popular platforms for meme coin trading:

Binance Stop Loss Setup

  1. Open the trading page for your meme coin pair (e.g., DOGE/USDT, BONK/USDT)
  2. Select "Stop-Limit" from the order types menu
  3. Set your Stop price: The price at which the order activates
  4. Set your Limit price: The price you're willing to sell at (typically 1-2% below stop for urgent exits)
  5. Set your Quantity: How much of your position to sell
  6. Click "Sell" to activate the stop loss

Bybit Stop Loss Setup

  1. Go to the Spot trading page for your meme coin pair
  2. Click "Conditional Order"
  3. Select "Stop Loss"
  4. Enter your trigger price and quantity
  5. Choose Market or Limit execution
  6. Confirm the order

Kraken Stop Loss Setup

  1. Open the trading terminal for your pair
  2. Click "Stop Loss" order type
  3. Enter the stop price and amount
  4. Optional: Set a limit price for stop-limit orders
  5. Place the order
💡 Pro Tip: On CEXs, set your stop loss 5-8% below support levels, not at round numbers. Round numbers ($0.10, $0.05) are where liquidity clusters, and prices often wick through them before bouncing.

How to Set Stop Loss Orders on Solana DEXs (Jupiter, Raydium, Orca)

Most meme coins trade on Solana DEXs before hitting CEXs. Setting stop losses on-chain is different from CEXs — here's exactly how to do it:

Method 1: Using Jupiter Limit Orders as Stop Losses

Jupiter Exchange offers limit orders that can function as stop losses:

  1. Go to Jupiter Exchange and connect your Phantom/Solflare wallet
  2. Select the "Limit Order" tab
  3. Choose your token pair (e.g., BONK → USDC)
  4. Set the trigger price below the current market price (this acts as your stop)
  5. Enter the amount you want to sell
  6. Review and confirm the transaction in your wallet

Note: Jupiter limit orders may not fill during extreme volatility. For critical exits, use a faster method.

Method 2: Telegram Trading Bots with Stop Loss

Telegram bots like Trojan, Maestro, and Banana Gun offer built-in stop loss functionality for Solana meme coins:

  1. Add the bot to your Telegram and set up your wallet
  2. When buying a token, set your Auto Sell % (stop loss) and Take Profit %
  3. The bot will automatically execute sells when either target is hit
  4. You can also set trailing stop losses through the bot's settings
⚠ Security Warning: Only use well-established Telegram trading bots with audited smart contracts. Never give seed phrases to any bot. Use a dedicated trading wallet with limited funds for bot trading. Read our crypto scam prevention guide for safety tips.

Method 3: Manual Stop Loss Tracking with Alerts

For traders who prefer manual exits:

  1. Set price alerts on DexScreener or Birdeye at your stop loss level
  2. When the alert triggers, manually sell through your wallet
  3. Keep your trading wallet open and ready during active trading hours

Take Profit Strategies: Where to Set Your Sell Targets

Taking profit is harder than setting a stop loss. Greed tells you to hold for 100x. Fear tells you to sell at the first green candle. Here's a systematic approach to take profit levels:

The 3-Tier Take Profit Framework

① TP1: 25-50% Gain

Sell 25% of position

Lock in initial gains. Covers your entry cost plus profit. After this, the trade is playing with house money.

② TP2: 100-200% Gain (2x-3x)

Sell 25-35% of position

Major profit-taking level. Most meme coins will retrace significantly after doubling.

③ TP3: 500%+ Gain (5x+)

Sell remaining 40-50%

Let the rest ride with a trailing stop. If it hits, you've captured life-changing gains. If not, you've already locked in 2-3x on your full position.

How to Identify Take Profit Levels Using Technical Analysis

  1. Resistance Levels: Look at historical price action. Previous highs, round numbers, and order book walls are natural take profit zones. Learn more in our support and resistance guide.
  2. Market Cap Targets: For meme coins, use market cap ranges:
    • Small cap ($1M-$10M): 5x-10x potential, but high risk of going to zero
    • Mid cap ($10M-$100M): 2x-5x potential, moderate risk
    • Large cap ($100M+): 1.5x-3x potential, lower risk
  3. Fibonacci Extensions: The 161.8% and 261.8% Fibonacci levels often act as price magnets during pumps.
  4. Volume Analysis: When daily trading volume exceeds the coin's market cap, distribution is likely happening — time to take profits.
💡 Pro Tip: Never set your take profit at an exact round number (like $0.01 or $0.10). Place it slightly below — at $0.0098 or $0.095 — to avoid getting caught in the liquidity cluster where smart money sells into buy orders.

Trailing Stop Loss: The Secret Weapon for Capturing 100x Moves

The problem with fixed take profits is that you can miss out on massive gains. A meme coin might hit your 100% take profit, you sell... and then it goes to 50x without you.

Trailing stops solve this.

How Trailing Stops Work

A trailing stop is set at a fixed percentage below the highest price the coin has reached since you bought. As the price rises, the stop moves up with it. If the price drops by your trail percentage, the stop triggers and you exit.

Trail %Best ForExample
10-15%Fast-moving meme coins with high volatilityBuy at $0.01. Price hits $0.05. Stop is at $0.0425 (15% below peak). Price drops to $0.0425 = exit with 325% gain.
20-30%Ultra-volatile penny coinsGives room for 20-30% wicks that are common in low-liquidity tokens.
5-8%Established coins on CEXsTighter trailing for BTC, ETH, SOL, and larger cap meme coins like DOGE or SHIB.

Setting Up Trailing Stops on Different Platforms

  • Binance: Available in the Advanced Trading interface. Set trail % and activate.
  • Bybit: Conditional orders with trailing stop option. Available for spot and derivatives.
  • Telegram Bots (Trojan, Maestro): Set "Trailing Take Profit" when buying. The bot automatically adjusts your sell price as the token pumps.
  • Manual: Track your position's peak price using DexScreener or CoinGecko alerts and manually adjust your stop as price rises.

Position Sizing + Stop Loss: The Complete Risk Management Formula

Stop losses and position sizing work together. Here's the exact formula professional traders use:

The Kelly-Inspired Formula for Meme Coins

Position Size = (Portfolio Value x Risk %) / (Entry Price - Stop Loss Price)

Example:

  • Portfolio: $10,000
  • Risk per trade: 2% ($200)
  • Entry price: $0.05
  • Stop loss price: $0.04 (20% below entry)
  • Position Size = $200 / ($0.05 - $0.04) = $200 / $0.01 = 20,000 tokens ($1,000)

This means you buy $1,000 worth of tokens. If your stop loss at $0.04 is hit, you lose exactly $200 (2% of your portfolio).

💡 Pro Tip: For meme coins, set your stop loss wider than you think is necessary — 20-30% is standard because 10-15% wicks are normal in these markets. Compensate by reducing your position size proportionally.

5 Deadly Stop Loss Mistakes Meme Coin Traders Make

❌ Mistake 1: Setting Stop Losses Too Tight

Setting a 5% stop loss on a token that regularly moves 20% intraday means you'll get stopped out before the real move happens. Fix: Use ATR (Average True Range) to set stops. Multiply the coin's average daily range by 1.5-2x and place your stop below that.

❌ Mistake 2: Moving Your Stop Loss Down

This is the most common mistake. Price drops to your stop, but you move it lower "just to see if it bounces." It doesn't bounce. You now have a 50% loss instead of a 10% loss. Fix: Treat stop losses as sacred. Never move them lower.

❌ Mistake 3: No Stop Loss on "Safe" Positions

"I don't need a stop on Bitcoin or Solana — they always bounce back." This is dangerous thinking. Every position needs a stop. Even SOL dropped 50%+ multiple times in 2025-2026. Fix: Every trade gets a stop loss, period.

❌ Mistake 4: Not Accounting for Slippage

On Solana DEXs with low liquidity, your market stop order might fill 10-20% below your stop price during a flash crash. Fix: Set your trigger price higher than your acceptable loss to account for slippage, or use stop-limit orders.

❌ Mistake 5: Ignoring Weekend and Holiday Gaps

Meme coins trade 24/7. Your stop loss might trigger at 3 AM Sunday when liquidity is thin and slippage is massive. Fix: Account for low-liquidity periods by widening stops on weekends.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I set a stop loss on Solana DEXs without a Telegram bot?

Yes. Use Jupiter's limit orders set below market price. However, they may not fill during extreme volatility. For guaranteed exits, Telegram bots like Trojan or Maestro offer more reliable stop loss execution on Solana meme coins.

What's the best stop loss percentage for meme coins?

For low-cap meme coins ($1M-$10M market cap), use 25-35%. For mid-cap ($10M-$100M), use 15-25%. For large-cap ($100M+), use 10-15%. Always account for the coin's average daily range — if it moves 30% daily, a 15% stop will get hit constantly.

Should I use a stop loss or a stop limit on volatile coins?

Use a market stop loss for volatile meme coins. Yes, you might get 5-10% slippage, but a stop-limit order might never fill during a flash crash. In crypto, survival depends on getting out, not getting the best price.

How do I set a trailing stop loss on Phantom wallet?

Phantom wallet itself doesn't support trailing stops. You need to use either a CEX (Binance, Bybit) or a Telegram trading bot (Trojan, Maestro) that offers trailing stop functionality.

What's the minimum amount I should trade with stop losses?

Any amount. Even $50 trades should have stop losses. The principle scales: risk 1-2% of whatever you're trading with. Small accounts grow by protecting capital, not by making 100x bets.

Do stop losses work during flash crashes?

Market stop losses work — but you may experience significant slippage. During the March 2026 flash crash, some Solana meme coins saw 40-60% slippage on stop orders. To mitigate this, use stop-limit orders with a wide limit, or reduce position sizes before major events (FOMC, CPI reports).
