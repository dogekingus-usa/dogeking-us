Frequently Asked Questions

What is a meme coin presale?

A meme coin presale is an early token sale that happens before the project lists on decentralized exchanges (DEXs). Participants buy tokens at the lowest possible price, before public trading begins. If the project succeeds, early presale buyers can see massive returns at listing.

How much can you make from a meme coin presale?

Returns vary dramatically. Top presales in 2026 are projecting 20x-266x returns at listing. However, many presales fail or turn out to be scams. Only invest what you can afford to lose, and always do thorough research.

What's the minimum investment for a presale?

Most Solana-based presales have minimum contributions of 0.1-1 SOL ($15-$150). The maximum is often capped per wallet to prevent whales from dominating the allocation.

How do I find new presales before they sell out?

Follow crypto launchpad Twitter accounts, join Telegram groups dedicated to presale alerts, and use platforms like Jupiter and Raydium's launch sections. The DogeKing Bundle includes a real-time presale monitor that alerts you the moment new presales go live.

Are presale tokens safe?

Presales carry significant risk. However, you can minimize risk by investing only in projects with audited smart contracts, locked liquidity, doxxed teams, and transparent tokenomics. Never invest based solely on hype or Telegram group pressure.

When can I sell presale tokens?

It depends on the project's tokenomics. Some presales distribute tokens immediately at TGE with no lockup. Others have a vesting schedule (e.g., 20% at TGE, then linear unlock over 6 months). Always check the token unlock schedule before investing.

What is a fair launch vs. a presale?

A fair launch distributes tokens equally to all participants with no special treatment for early investors. A presale gives early birds a discounted price before the public launch. For a detailed breakdown, see our guide to token launch strategies on Solana.
