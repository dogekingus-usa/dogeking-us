DeFi Aggregators on Solana: How Jupiter, Step Finance, and Sonar Watch Simplify Your Portfolio Management

By Samuel Pason · DogeKing Editor

Published: May 26, 2026 | Category: DeFi Aggregators & Portfolio Management | Reading Time: 12 min

The Solana DeFi ecosystem in 2026 sprawls across dozens of protocols. You might hold LP tokens on Raydium, staked SOL on Marinade, lendable assets on Kamino, and perp positions on Zeta - all from the same wallet. Tacking on swap transactions through Jupiter, yield-farming on Orca, and airdrop claims turns managing your positions into a data management nightmare.

Opening five dashboards just to check your net worth is not sustainable. Manually exporting CSV files from each protocol for tax season is even worse. This is where DeFi aggregators enter - platforms that ingest on-chain data from every protocol your wallet has touched and present a unified, real-time portfolio view.

In this guide, we break down the four major Solana DeFi aggregators in 2026: Jupiter Portfolio, Step Finance, Sonar Watch, and Apeboard. We cover what each platform does best, how they handle multi-protocol tracking, tax reporting integrations, and how to combine them for a complete portfolio management setup.

Key Insight: The best Solana DeFi portfolio setup in 2026 uses two aggregators in parallel - one for real-time analytics and alerts (Sonar Watch or Step Finance) and one for tax-ready transaction history (Jupiter Portfolio or Apeboard). No single tool does everything perfectly, but a two-tool stack covers all bases.

Jupiter Portfolio: Swap History Meets Position Tracking

Jupiter has long been the dominant swap aggregator on Solana. In 2026, its portfolio features have matured into a full-fledged portfolio management layer that goes far beyond swap history.

Jupiter Portfolio connects to your wallet and provides a live breakdown of every asset and position - not just raw token balances but also LP positions, staked assets, and lending positions across Raydium, Orca, Kamino, Marinade, and Sanctum.

What Jupiter Portfolio Tracks

  • Token balances - real-time USD value of every token in your wallet, sorted by allocation percentage.
  • LP positions - your share of liquidity pools on Raydium, Orca, Meteora, and other AMMs, including earned fees and impermanent loss estimates.
  • Staked positions - staked SOL through Marinade (mSOL), Jito (JitoSOL), Blaze (bSOL), and Sanctum's infinite LP framework.
  • Lending positions - supplied and borrowed assets on Kamino, Marginfi, and Solend, including health factor and liquidation thresholds.
  • Perp positions - open futures positions on Zeta Markets and Drift, showing entry price, liquidation price, and unrealized P&L.
  • Swap history - every swap executed through Jupiter, with USD value, slippage, route, and DEX breakdowns.

The Tax-Grade Export

Jupiter Portfolio's standout feature for serious traders is its CSV export for tax reporting. The export includes every trade categorized by type (swap, add liquidity, remove liquidity, stake, unstake, lend, borrow) with fill prices, timestamps, and USD equivalents at the time of transaction. This export is compatible with major crypto tax platforms including CoinLedger, Koinly, and Cryptiony.

Pro Tip: Jupiter's CSV export uses the same timestamp format and column schema as CoinLedger's import template. You can export your full Solana transaction history from Jupiter Portfolio and upload it directly to CoinLedger without any reformatting. This alone saves 2-3 hours of manual CSV wrangling during tax season.

One limitation to note: Jupiter Portfolio is Solana-only. If you hold assets on Ethereum, Base, Arbitrum, or other chains, Jupiter will not see them. This makes it an excellent Solana-specific portfolio manager but not a complete cross-chain solution.

Step Finance: The Comprehensive Solana Dashboard

Step Finance is the most comprehensive DeFi dashboard on Solana. If Jupiter is laser-focused on swaps and positions, Step is the panoramic view - connect any wallet and it auto-indexes every protocol interaction into tokens, LP positions, staking, lending, NFTs, and airdrop claims.

Dashboard Modules

  • Portfolio Overview - total wallet value across all asset classes, with 24h change, 7d change, and all-time P&L.
  • LP Positions - detailed breakdown of every liquidity pool you are in, with APR estimates, earned fees, and deposit/withdrawal history.
  • Staking Positions - stake accounts and liquid staking derivatives tracked with real-time yields.
  • Debt Positions - loans and borrows across lending protocols with health factors and liquidation warnings.
  • NFT Portfolio - floor price tracking and collection-level valuation for your Solana NFTs.
  • Transaction History - searchable, filterable log of every transaction, categorized by protocol and type.
  • Yield Optimizer - Step Finance suggests optimal yield strategies based on your current holdings and positions.

Multi-Wallet Support

Step Finance supports tracking multiple wallets under a single dashboard view. This is essential for traders who manage separate wallets for trading, long-term holding, and farming. You can add up to 10 wallets in the free tier and unlimited wallets with Step's premium subscription (5 SOL/month).

You will need to filter the data slightly before uploading to a tax platform. For most traders, it is still far faster than building the CSV from scratch.

Sonar Watch: Real-Time Analytics and Portfolio Alerts

Where Jupiter and Step Finance focus on historical tracking, Sonar Watch specializes in real-time analytics - the tool you open when you want to know what is happening right now. It provides a live dashboard tracking every token price, LP value, and staking yield in real time, with a killer alerting engine.

Alerting and Notifications

Set custom price alerts and position-level triggers for LP value changes or lending health factor drops. Alerts arrive via Telegram, Discord, email, or in-app. For leveraged traders on Kamino or Marginfi, configure a 1.5x health factor alert to add collateral before a liquidation event.

Portfolio Analytics

  • P&L tracking - realized and unrealized profit/loss filterable by date range and protocol.
  • Exposure breakdown - allocation percentage by token, protocol, and asset class (spot, LP, staked, borrowed).
  • Impermanent loss calculator - real-time IL estimate for every LP position vs. holding the underlying tokens.
  • Gas tracker - average daily SOL spent on fees, broken down by protocol.
  • Whale wallet tracking - monitor any wallet and get notified on large moves.

Pro Tip: Use Sonar Watch's whale tracking to monitor known market maker wallets or early investor wallets for tokens you hold. When a whale wallet moves tokens to a CEX deposit address, you get a notification before the price impact hits the order book. This is one of the most underused alpha sources in Solana DeFi.

Apeboard: Cross-Chain Portfolio Consolidation

If you hold positions on Ethereum, Base, or Arbitrum alongside your Solana portfolio, Apeboard bridges the gap. It supports 12+ blockchains, presenting a unified net worth with per-chain breakdowns in a single UI.

Cross-Chain Features

  • Unified net worth - total portfolio value across Solana, Ethereum, Base, Arbitrum, Polygon, Optimism, Avalanche, BNB Chain, and more.
  • Per-chain breakdown - see exactly how much value is on each chain and which protocols are generating the most exposure.
  • Cross-chain LP tracking - Apeboard indexes LP positions on Uniswap V2/V3 (Ethereum/Base/Arbitrum), Raydium (Solana), and PancakeSwap (BNB Chain) in a single view.
  • Transaction export - all-chain transaction history exportable as CSV for tax reporting.
  • Bridge tracking - Apeboard detects cross-chain bridge transactions and marks them as transfers rather than taxable disposals, which is critical for accurate tax reporting.

Portfolio Tracking Across Protocols: Making Sense of the Data

The promise of DeFi aggregation is a single source of truth. The reality is more nuanced - each aggregator indexes protocols differently, uses different pricing oracles, and categorizes positions with different taxonomies.

The Indexing Gap

Step Finance has the broadest coverage at 450+ protocols. Jupiter Portfolio covers ~60 protocols with deeper data for swaps and LP positions. Sonar Watch focuses on the top 30 protocols by TVL. Apeboard covers major Solana protocols plus cross-chain equivalents. A protocol not indexed simply will not appear in your view - check coverage before relying on any single aggregator.

Pricing Divergence

Each aggregator sources prices from different oracles - Jupiter uses its own DEX-aggregated API, Step Finance uses Pyth and Switchboard, Sonar Watch uses Jupiter and Pyth, Apeboard uses Chainlink for EVM chains. These can diverge by 1-3% during volatility. For tax reporting, use the prices from the aggregator whose export you are submitting.

Practical Multi-Protocol Workflow

Here is the portfolio management workflow used by experienced Solana DeFi traders in 2026:

  • Daily: Sonar Watch for real-time price alerts, health factor monitoring, and whale wallet tracking.
  • Weekly: Step Finance dashboard for full portfolio overview including LP fee earnings and yield changes.
  • Monthly: Jupiter Portfolio export for swap-centric P&L analysis and reconciliation.
  • Quarterly/Tax Season: Export from Jupiter Portfolio (Solana) and Apeboard (cross-chain) for tax filing.

Choosing Your Aggregator Stack

Pure Solana Trader

If 90%+ of your portfolio is on Solana, the optimal stack is Jupiter Portfolio + Sonar Watch - Jupiter for swap history, position tracking, and tax exports; Sonar Watch for real-time alerts and whale tracking.

Multi-Protocol Solana Farmer

Farming across Raydium, Orca, Kamino, and Marinade? Use Step Finance as your primary dashboard for 450+ protocol coverage and NFT tracking, supplemented by Sonar Watch for real-time health factor alerts.

Cross-Chain Investor

Holding positions on Solana, Ethereum, Base, and Arbitrum? Use Apeboard for the unified cross-chain view and Jupiter Portfolio for Solana-specific tax exports.

Bottom Line: Spend 30 minutes setting up your aggregator stack now. The cost of premium tiers (2-5 SOL/month) is negligible compared to the time savings and the cost of a missed liquidation or tax filing error.

How to Set Up Your Aggregator Stack in 10 Minutes

  1. Connect your wallet to Jupiter Portfolio at portfolio.jup.ag - it auto-indexes your entire position history.
  2. Export your swap history as CSV and verify the data matches expectations. Discrepancies usually mean a protocol is not fully indexed.
  3. Set up Sonar Watch alerts - connect Telegram or Discord, set price alerts for your top 5 positions, and configure a health factor alert at 1.5x if you use leverage.
  4. Add your wallets to Step Finance for multi-wallet consolidated net worth.
  5. If cross-chain, connect to Apeboard - add EVM wallet addresses and verify bridge transactions are correctly categorized.
  6. Set a monthly calendar reminder to export your Jupiter Portfolio CSV for P&L tracking. Consistent exports beat reconstructing 365 days in April.
