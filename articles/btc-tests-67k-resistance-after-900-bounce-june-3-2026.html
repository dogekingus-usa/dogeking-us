The $900 Bounce: What Happened

Bitcoin has mounted a sharp $900 relief bounce from today's low of $66,301, reaching $67,199 as of 2:20 PM ICT. After spending over 4 hours compressed in a tight $66.3K range (Articles #321 and #322), BTC finally broke upward ” but now faces the critical question: Can $67K hold as support, or will sellers reject this rally?

By Samuel Pason · DogeKing Editor

The bounce was preceded by a clear pattern of exhaustion selling. Volume surged during the initial selloff this morning (BTC broke below $66.5K to $66,301 at ~10:45 AM ICT), then tapered as sellers ran out of steam. The extended consolidation at $66.3K built a base, and the breakout above $66.5K triggered short squeezes that accelerated the move to $67K.

Critical Level: $67K as Resistance

The $67,000 level was former support that broke overnight when BTC dropped from $68K+ to $66K. In technical analysis, former support becomes new resistance ” and that's exactly the dynamic playing out now.

Volume Profile: What the Data Says

The bounce came on elevated volume ” a positive sign that suggests genuine buying interest rather than a low-volume dead cat bounce. However, total crypto market volume is up 17% on the day, indicating active capital repositioning rather than pure accumulation.

Key volume observations:

  • Bounce volume > selloff volume at the $66.3K consolidation ” bullish divergence
  • Spot buying > futures buying ” suggests organic demand, not leveraged speculation
  • BTC dominance steady at ~56% ” indicates the move is BTC-led, not altcoin-led (yet)
