Total Supply & Distribution

$DKING launched with a fixed total supply of 1 billion tokens. Unlike tokens with uncapped supplies that suffer from infinite dilution, $DKING's supply can only decrease — never increase — because the mint authority has been renounced.

Initial Distribution

Why Renounced Mint Authority Matters

When a token's mint authority is renounced, no one — not the team, not the founder, not any external entity — can mint new tokens. This means:

  • ✅ No infinite inflation
  • ✅ No surprise dilution
  • ✅ Supply can only decrease (through burns)
  • ✅ Scarcity increases over time

This is a critical trust signal that separates legitimate projects from pump-and-dump schemes where the team retains the ability to mint unlimited tokens.

The 1% Burn Mechanism

The heart of $DKING's deflationary design is the 1% per-transaction burn. Here's how it works:

How the Burn Works

  1. Every time $DKING is transferred, bought, or sold on-chain, 1% of the transaction amount is permanently removed from circulation.
  2. The burned tokens are sent to the Solana burn address — a wallet with no known private key, making the tokens irretrievable.
  3. The remaining 99% is processed as a normal transaction.

Burn Scenarios

  • Normal trade (1,000 $DKING): 10 $DKING burned, 990 $DKING transferred
  • Weekly burn event (100,000 $DKING volume): 1,000 $DKING permanently removed
  • High-volume day (10M $DKING traded): 100,000 $DKING burned — accelerating the deflationary effect

Compounding Effect Over Time

As volume increases and circulating supply decreases, the same 1% burn removes a progressively larger percentage of remaining supply. This creates a compound deflationary effect:

  • Year 1: ~5-10% of total supply burned through organic volume
  • Year 2: Same volume burns a larger percentage of remaining supply as total supply shrinks
  • Year 5: Estimated 20-35% of initial supply could be permanently removed from circulation
⚠ Important: Burn rates depend entirely on on-chain volume. Higher adoption and trading activity accelerate the burn. Lower volume slows it. The burn is a feature of the token contract — it operates automatically regardless of market conditions.

Deflationary Economics

$DKING is designed as a deflationary asset. Unlike fiat currencies or uncapped cryptocurrencies that lose purchasing power through inflation, $DKING becomes scarcer over time.

Simple Supply Math

  • Initial supply: 1,000,000,000 $DKING
  • After 5% burned: 950,000,000 $DKING — effective price floor rises 5.2%
  • After 15% burned: 850,000,000 $DKING — each remaining token represents a larger share of market cap
  • After 30% burned: 700,000,000 $DKING — supply reduced by nearly a third

What This Means for Holders

Even if the market cap stays flat, a decreasing supply means each token's value increases proportionally. A holder who owns 1% of the supply today will own an increasingly large effective share as tokens are burned — without buying a single additional token.
