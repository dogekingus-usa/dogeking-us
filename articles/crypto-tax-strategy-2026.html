Crypto Tax Strategy 2026: Save Money with Tax-Loss Harvesting & Reporting

By Samuel Pason · DogeKing Editor

Crypto Tax Basics for 2026

In the US, crypto is taxed as property by the IRS. Key events: selling crypto for fiat (capital gains), trading one crypto for another (taxable event), earning crypto as income (ordinary income), spending crypto (capital gains), and airdrops (ordinary income). Short-term gains (held <1 year) taxed at ordinary income rates up to 37%. Long-term gains (held >1 year) taxed at 0-20%.

Tax-Loss Harvesting Strategy

Tax-loss harvesting is the single most effective legal strategy to reduce crypto taxes. Sell losing positions before year-end to realize losses that offset gains. You can deduct up to $3,000 of net losses against ordinary income annually and carry forward unlimited losses. Pro tip: don't wait until December - harvest losses throughout the year when positions are down.

Cost Basis Methods: FIFO vs HIFO vs Specific ID

FIFO (First In, First Out) is the IRS default - you sell your oldest coins first, which may mean higher gains if you bought early. HIFO (Highest In, First Out) sells the highest-cost-basis coins first, minimizing gains. Specific ID lets you choose which lots to sell - most tax-efficient. Software like CoinTracking and Koinly supports Specific ID.

DeFi Tax Reporting Challenges

DeFi creates complex tax events: providing liquidity (potentially taxable when LP tokens are minted/redeemed), yield farming (rewards taxed as income when received), staking (rewards taxed as income), and lending (interest taxed as income). Use dedicated DeFi tax software like Koinly or CoinLedger that integrates with wallet addresses and tracks all transactions.

Maintain detailed records: purchase date/time, amount, cost basis, sale date, proceeds, and purpose (investment/spending).
