2 Set Up Your Journal Categories

Create separate tabs or sections for:

  • Trade Log: Every trade with all fields above
  • Monthly Summary: Win rate, P&L, biggest win/loss, total fees
  • Pattern Recognition: What setups are winning? What times of day?
  • Mistakes Log: Track every mistake you make to eliminate them
  • Strategy Performance: Compare scalping vs. swing results

3 Pre-Trade Checklist (Before Every Entry)

Run through this checklist before opening a position:

  • •… Have I checked whale activity on this token?
  • •… Do I have a clear entry and exit plan?
  • •… What's my stop loss level?
  • •… Am I trading or gambling right now?
  • •… Have I checked my slippage settings?
  • •… Is my position size within my risk limits?

Fill out the entry section of your journal. If you can't answer all questions, don't trade.

4 Record Every Exit Immediately

As soon as a trade closes, fill out the exit section. Don't wait ” you'll forget the emotional context, which is the most valuable data. Include:

  • Exact exit price and time
  • Exit reason (truthfully ” was it planned or emotional?)
  • Your emotional state at exit
  • The one lesson you're taking away

5 Weekly Review Session

Block 30 minutes every Sunday to review your journal. Ask:

  • What patterns are winning? (time of day, trade type, token type)
  • What patterns are losing? (be ruthless ” cut what doesn't work)
  • Am I following my rules? (if not, why?)
  • What's my win rate this week? (target: 55%+ on 2:1 risk/reward)
  • How much did fees and slippage cost me?

5 Common Trading Journal Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

Mistake #1: Only Journaling Losing Trades

It's tempting to only analyze losses, but winning trades contain just as much valuable data. What did you do RIGHT on your best trades? Journal everything.

Mistake #2: Being Too Vague

"The chart looked bullish" is useless. Be specific: "Breakout above resistance at $0.00012 with 3x average volume and whale buy orders detected on DexScreener." Specificity creates actionable patterns.

Mistake #3: Not Journaling Emotions

The numbers tell you what happened, but the emotions tell you WHY. If you journal "FOMO: bought at the top because I saw a 5x tweet," you'll learn to recognize that feeling and stop yourself next time.

Mistake #4: Abandoning the Journal After 2 Weeks

Consistency is everything. You need at least 50-100 journaled trades to see meaningful patterns. Treat journaling like brushing your teeth ” non-negotiable.

Mistake #5: Not Reviewing the Data

Journaling without weekly review is just record-keeping. Schedule a 30-minute session every Sunday. Review, analyze, adjust. The review is where the profit lives.
