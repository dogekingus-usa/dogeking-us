Cryptocurrency Security Checklist: 20 Steps to Protect Your Solana Portfolio from All Attack Vectors in 2026

By Samuel Pason · DogeKing Editor

Published: May 26, 2026 · Category: Solana Security · Reading time: 12 minutes

The Solana ecosystem has grown past $12 billion in DeFi TVL, with millions of daily active wallets trading, staking, and lending. The same growth that makes Solana the most active blockchain by transactions also makes it the most targeted by attackers. In 2025 alone, phishing attacks on Solana users increased by 340%, SIM-swap-related wallet drains rose 280%, and rogue dApp approvals accounted for over $47 million in losses.

Every Solana portfolio is a target - but a properly secured portfolio is close to impenetrable. This checklist covers 20 actionable steps across 10 security domains. Each step is a discrete action with a measurable outcome. Skip none. Your future self will thank you when the next phishing campaign hits your inbox.

20 Security steps in this checklist

Domain 1: Hardware Wallet Setup

1
Purchase a hardware wallet from the official manufacturer - never second-hand or resold

Hardware wallets from unauthorized resellers or second-hand markets are the most common entry point for pre-compromised devices. Buy directly from Ledger's official Amazon store or Trezor's website. When your device arrives, verify the anti-tamper seal is intact and run the genuine-check tool built into the wallet's firmware setup. A compromised hardware wallet can leak your seed phrase during generation - never use one whose supply chain you cannot verify.

2
Generate your seed phrase offline during initial setup - never in front of a camera or microphone

Modern phone cameras are good enough to read a seed phrase from 15 feet away. Generate your seed phrase in a room with no cameras, no smart speakers, and no window-facing desks. Cover your laptop camera with tape during setup. Smart home devices (Alexa, Google Home) are always listening - unplug them during seed generation. The 24-word recovery phrase is the master key to every wallet derived from it; treat its generation as you would treating nuclear launch codes.

3
Test your hardware wallet recovery before depositing meaningful funds

This single step catches more setup errors than any other. After generating your seed and PIN, reset the device to factory settings, then restore from the seed phrase. If the restore produces the same wallet addresses, your backup works. If it doesn't - and this happens more often than people admit - you caught the error with zero SOL at risk. Repeat this test every time you set up a new wallet or backup method.

Domain 2: Seed Phrase Security

4
Use steel backup plates - not paper - for long-term seed phrase storage

Paper burns in a house fire at 451Ã�š°F. Water soaks through paper within seconds. UV light fades ink in months. Steel backup plates (Billfodl, Cryptosteel, or even stamped titanium plates) survive fire, flood, and physical degradation. Punch the seed phrase into the metal plate using a letter-and-number stamp set. Store it in a fireproof safe. The one-time cost ($30-$100) is trivial compared to the cost of losing a five-figure Solana portfolio to a flood or house fire.

5
Distribute your seed phrase across two geographically separate locations

A single safe can be stolen or destroyed. Split your 24-word seed phrase into two 12-word halves (BIP39 allows any split scheme) and store each half in a different location - for example, half in your home safe, half in a safe deposit box at a bank 50 miles away. Each half is useless to an attacker alone. Use a passphrase (25th word) as an additional layer: the passphrase is memorized (never written down), and without it the seed alone creates an empty wallet. This is the gold standard for portfolio protection.

6
Never type, photograph, or digitally store your seed phrase - ever

Screenshots sync to iCloud and Google Photos. Typed seed phrases linger in clipboard history, text message backups, and cloud-based keyboard logs. Password managers storing seed phrases are one data breach away from exposure. Write the seed phrase on paper only during the initial setup, then immediately transfer to your steel backup. Delete any photos, text files, or email drafts containing seed phrase fragments. If you have ever photographed your seed phrase, assume it is compromised and migrate to a fresh wallet.

Domain 3: Wallet Approvals & Token Permissions

7
Revoke all unused token approvals monthly

Every dApp you interact with requests token spending approval. Over months, the accumulated approvals can exceed 50+ with dozens of protocols you no longer use. A compromised or malicious dApp can drain any asset you have approved - even if you never visit the dApp again. Use Solscan's Token Approval Checker, Step Finance's approval dashboard, or RugCheck.xyz to view all active approvals on your wallet. Revoke any approval for protocols you do not interact with at least weekly. Make this a calendar reminder on the first of every month.

8
Use a burner wallet for dApp interactions and a cold wallet for storage

The single most effective security pattern in crypto: separate your "hot" interaction wallet from your "cold" storage wallet. The hot wallet holds only enough SOL for gas fees and active trading (never more than 10% of your portfolio). The cold wallet - connected only to your hardware device - holds the rest. If the hot wallet is drained through a rogue approval, the loss is capped at 10%. This pattern alone would have prevented 90%+ of the individual user losses from the large dApp exploits of 2025.

Monthly Approval Audit: Connect only your hot wallet to Solscan's approval checker. Any approval lasting more than 90 days without use should be revoked. Any approval for a protocol that has been exploited should be revoked immediately - even if the protocol claims to have patched.

Domain 4: Phishing Prevention

9
Verify every URL before connecting your wallet - bookmark the real ones

Phishing sites use visually identical domains: jupitar.ag instead of jupitar.ag, marginfi.finance with a Cyrillic 'a' that renders identically to the Latin 'a'. Before connecting your wallet to any dApp, verify the domain name character by character. Bookmark the genuine URLs for every protocol you use - Jupiter (jupitar.ag), Phantom (phantom.app), Solflare (solflare.com), Marginfi (marginfi.com), Kamino (kamino.finance). Never click dApp links from Twitter DMs, Telegram groups, or Google ads. Type the URL manually or use your bookmark.

10
Install and use wallet-blocking browser extensions

Extensions like Wallet Guard and Phantom's built-in phishing detector block known malicious domains and flag suspicious dApp connection requests before you approve them. Wallet Guard specifically maintains a real-time blacklist of phishing domains used by crypto drainers. When combined with the open-source EtherAddressLookup extension (works with Solana too), these tools catch 95%+ of phishing sites before your eyes even see them. Run these extensions in addition to - not instead of - manual URL verification.

11
Never sign blind transactions - always decode the message first

This is the #1 cause of wallet drain in 2026. A dApp asks you to sign a transaction that looks legitimate in the wallet popup but contains hidden instructions transferring your SOL or SPL tokens to an attacker's address. Always use Phantom's "Advanced Details" toggle or Solflare's "Simulate Transaction" feature to view the raw instruction data. If the transaction includes a transfer or transferChecked instruction you did not explicitly trigger, reject it. Scammers rely on you clicking "Approve" without reading - do not be that statistic.

Critical: As of early 2026, a new class of "session hijacking" phishing attacks uses wallet connection requests that look like standard dApp connections but include hidden approve-all permissions. These affect Phantom, Backpack, and Solflare. Always check the permissions list in your wallet extension before confirming any connection.

Domain 5: SIM Swap Protection

12
Remove SMS as a 2FA method on every crypto-related account

SMS-based 2FA is the weakest link in security. A SIM-swap attack - where an attacker convinces your mobile carrier to transfer your number to their SIM - bypasses SMS 2FA in under 10 minutes. Every major exchange (Coinbase, Binance, Kraken) and email provider supports TOTP authenticator apps or hardware security keys. Replace SMS 2FA with Authy or Google Authenticator (TOTP) at minimum. Set up an account PIN with your mobile carrier that must be provided for any SIM change - this is a free phone call and blocks 80% of SIM-swap attempts.

13
Use a hardware security key (FIDO2/WebAuthn) for exchange accounts

A hardware security key like the YubiKey 5C NFC is phishing-resistant by design. Unlike TOTP codes that can be intercepted in real-time with an evilginx proxy, FIDO2 authentication requires physical possession of the key. Register your YubiKey as the primary 2FA method on Coinbase, Binance.US, and Kraken. Buy a second YubiKey as a backup and store it in a separate physical location. The $55 investment protects exchange accounts containing potentially hundreds of thousands of dollars.

Domain 6: dApp Permission Management

14
Audit your connected dApps every week and disconnect unused ones

Every dApp you connect to receives permission to view your wallet address and, depending on the connection type, request transactions. A compromised dApp frontend - even one that was legitimate - can use your active connection to prompt wallet drains. Phantom and Solflare show connected sites in their settings panel. Disconnect every dApp you have not used in the past 7 days. For long-term holds, keep zero dApps connected. Reconnect only when you are about to interact. The inconvenience of reconnecting is vastly smaller than the cost of a wipe.

15
Check dApp contract addresses against verified sources

Fake dApps deploy contracts with names identical to real protocols but use different program IDs. Before approving a transaction, verify the program ID against the official protocol documentation. Jupiter's official program ID is permanently listed on their docs site. Marinade's staking program is verified on Solscan. Third-party tools like RugCheck.xyz and SolSniffer automatically verify contract authenticity and flag suspicious programs. If RugCheck shows "unverified" for a contract claiming to be a major protocol - do not interact.

Pro Tip: Create a text file or password manager entry with the verified program IDs of every protocol you use regularly. Cross-reference every transaction's program ID against this list before approving. This takes 10 seconds per transaction and prevents the most common exploit pattern in Solana DeFi.

Domain 7: Multi-Signature Wallets

16
Use a 2-of-3 multi-sig for any portfolio worth over $10,000

A single private key is a single point of failure. If that key is compromised - through malware, a keylogger, or physical theft - your entire portfolio is gone in minutes. Multi-signature wallets require multiple independent keys to authorize any transaction. Squads is the leading multi-sig platform on Solana, supporting 2-of-3, 3-of-5, and custom threshold configurations. For a personal portfolio, a 2-of-3 setup with keys on (1) your hardware wallet, (2) a second hardware wallet at a different location, and (3) a trusted family member's device provides excellent security without excessive operational friction. A single compromised key cannot drain funds - the attacker needs two keys and physical access to at least one hardware device.

17
Configure timelocks on multi-sig transactions

Squads V3 and other Solana multi-sig solutions support transaction timelocks - a mandatory waiting period between signature submission and execution. Set a minimum 24-hour timelock on any transaction exceeding 10% of your portfolio value. This creates a window to cancel malicious transactions if one of your keys is compromised. For institutional-sized portfolios ($100k+), configure a 72-hour timelock with email/SMS notifications for any pending transaction. Timelocks prevented an estimated $8 million in losses during the 2025 targeted wallet attacks against high-value Solana holders.

Domain 8: Emergency Response Plan

18
Create and practice your personal emergency response plan

When your wallet is being drained, every second counts. Having a pre-practiced plan can mean the difference between saving $50,000 and watching it disappear. Your plan should cover four scenarios:

(1) Active drain: If you see unauthorized outgoing transactions, immediately transfer remaining assets to a freshly generated wallet using a different device. Do not use the compromised wallet to send - use a clean, never-connected wallet address and transfer from exchange withdrawal. (2) Seed phrase suspected compromised: Generate a new wallet on a hardware device, transfer all assets immediately, never use the compromised seed again. (3) Physical theft of hardware wallet: If your Ledger or Trezor is stolen, you have time as long as the PIN was strong - most devices wipe after 10 incorrect attempts. Move funds to a new wallet within 24 hours. (4) Lost access (forgotten PIN, damaged device): Use your steel backup seed plate and a new hardware device to restore. If the seed plate is also lost, you have a bigger problem - which is why step 5 (geographic distribution) is critical.

Write each scenario's procedure on a physical card stored near your emergency supplies. Practice the sequence once per quarter.

19
Set up monitoring alerts for your wallet addresses

Automated monitoring is your early warning system. Use Helius webhooks or QuickNode alerts to receive real-time notifications for any transaction above a threshold (e.g., >1 SOL transfer) from your wallet addresses. Configure Telegram, Discord, or email notifications. Step Finance's portfolio tracker includes customizable alerts. Sonar Watch provides on-chain monitoring with push notifications. The goal is to know within 60 seconds - not 6 hours - if an unauthorized transaction occurs. For multi-sig wallets, configure alerts for all signature submissions, not just executed transactions.

Domain 9: Browser Extension Security

20
Use a dedicated browser profile exclusively for crypto - nothing else

Browser extensions are the single largest attack surface for self-custody wallets. A malicious browser extension - even one that was legitimate at install time but was later compromised - can read your wallet extension's permissions, intercept transaction signing prompts, and extract private keys from wallet extensions with poor isolation. Create a dedicated Chrome or Brave profile that is used only for crypto. Install exactly these extensions: your wallet (Phantom, Backpack, or Solflare), Wallet Guard, and - if needed - Metamask Snap for Solana. No ad blockers, no password managers, no grammar checkers, no VPN extensions, no coupon finders, no note-taking extensions. Each additional extension is a potential attack vector. The dedicated profile is a simple behavioral change that eliminates an entire class of risk.

Domain 10: Mobile Wallet Security

Mobile wallets are convenient and increasingly popular - Phantom and Solflare have over 8 million combined mobile installs. But phones present unique risks: they are stolen more often than laptops, run more third-party apps, and are susceptible to SMS-based attacks.

+
Enable biometric authentication and disable auto-lock timeout

Set your mobile wallet to require biometric authentication (face or fingerprint) for every transaction - not just wallet unlock. Phantom's mobile app supports Face ID/Touch ID for transaction signing. Set the app to lock immediately when backgrounded. Never enable "remember password" or extended session timeouts. If your phone is stolen while unlocked, you have minutes - not hours - to act, and biometric-gated transactions are your last line of defense.

+
Never install wallet apps from unofficial app stores or sideloaded APKs

Fake Phantom and Solflare apps appear on third-party Android stores and sideloading forums regularly. These look identical to the real apps but contain hardcoded address substitution - every transaction you send gets redirected to the attacker's wallet. Install wallet apps exclusively from the Apple App Store (iOS) or Google Play Store (Android). Verify the developer name: Phantom's official developer is "Phantom Technologies, Inc." and Solflare's is "Solflare." If the download count seems low or reviews mention stolen funds, flag it immediately.

+
Keep your mobile wallet balance under what you are comfortable losing

Mobile wallets are hot wallets by definition - the private key exists on an internet-connected device. Treat them as such. Keep only enough SOL for active trading, gas fees, and operational needs. The majority of your portfolio belongs on a hardware wallet or multi-sig. A good rule: if the thought of losing your phone makes you nervous about your crypto, you have too much in your mobile wallet. Move the excess to cold storage today.

Locking It Down: The 20-Step Checklist in Practice

Security is not a one-time configuration - it is an ongoing discipline. The 20 steps above fall into three cadences:

One-Time Setup (Steps 1-6): Purchase, generate, and secure your hardware wallet. Create steel backups. Distribute geographically. Set up your passphrase. These steps take one focused afternoon and provide the foundation for everything else.

Weekly Habits (Steps 7, 11, 14, 15): Revoke unused approvals. Audit dApp connections. Verify transaction details. Check RugCheck for new protocols. These take 10 minutes every Sunday and catch 90% of emerging threats before they hit you.

Monthly Review (Steps 7, 14, 19): Run a full approval scan. Disconnect every dApp. Review your monitoring alerts. Re-test your emergency plan mentally. These take 30 minutes on the first of each month and ensure your security posture stays current with the evolving threat landscape.

The attackers are automated, persistent, and creative. But the baseline defense is simple, repeatable, and - once established - nearly automatic. You do not need to be a security engineer to protect six figures in Solana assets. You just need to check these 20 boxes and maintain the habits.

Start with the hardware wallet. Do step 1 today. The rest follows.
