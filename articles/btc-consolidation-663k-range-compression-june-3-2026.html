The Compression Pattern: What It Means

Range compression ” also known as a "coiling" pattern ” occurs when an asset's price oscillates in an increasingly narrow band after a sharp directional move. The tighter the range, the more pent-up energy, and the more explosive the eventual breakout.

BTC's 2-hour range of just $60 (from $66,300 to $66,360) is exceptionally tight for Bitcoin. For context, Bitcoin's average 2-hour range during normal market conditions is approximately $150-$300. This compression tells us:

  • Sellers are temporarily exhausted ” the selling pressure that drove BTC from $67K to $66.3K has diminished
  • Buyers are hesitant ” no aggressive accumulation is happening at these levels, suggesting uncertainty about the bottom
  • The market is waiting for a catalyst ” either a macroeconomic trigger (FOMC positioning, equity market cues) or a technical signal (break of $66K or a reclaim of $66.5K)
Range compression is like a spring being wound tighter. The longer BTC stays in this $60-wide channel, the more explosive the next move will be ” in either direction.
