Solana DeFi Lending and Borrowing: Best Platforms for Earning Yield in 2026

Published: May 25, 2026 | Category: DeFi | Reading Time: 9 min

DeFi lending on Solana has evolved rapidly. In 2026, you can earn 6-15% APY on stablecoins, borrow against your crypto at competitive rates, and layer multiple yield strategies using liquid staking tokens. This guide covers the top lending platforms on Solana and how to use them effectively.

1. Kamino Lend ” Best Overall Lending Platform

Kamino has become Solana's dominant lending protocol in 2026. Its smart lending pools automatically optimize interest rates based on supply and demand, and its "Multiply" feature lets you leverage your positions within the same interface.

Key Features:

  • Supply SOL, USDC, USDT, jitoSOL, mSOL, and more at competitive rates
  • Borrow stablecoins at 4-8% APR depending on pool utilization
  • Leverage yield with up to 3x on LST-collateralized positions
  • Automated yield optimization ” no need to manually chase rates
  • Insurance fund protecting depositors against bad debt

2. Marginfi ” Best for Liquid Staking Token Lending

Marginfi specializes in lending against liquid staking tokens (LSTs) like jitoSOL, mSOL, and bSOL. Its group-based lending system separates different asset classes into distinct risk pools, protecting depositors from correlated defaults.

Key Features:

  • Lend jitoSOL at ~3.5% APY + staking rewards = ~11% total
  • Borrow stablecoins at competitive rates against LST collateral
  • Liquid Risk Tokens (LRTs) for advanced yield strategies
  • No admin keys ” fully immutable smart contracts
  • Isolated pools prevent contagion across asset classes

3. Solend ” Best for Beginners

Solend was Solana's first major lending protocol and remains popular for its clean interface and reliability. While its yields are slightly lower than Kamino's, Solend's longer track record makes it a trusted choice for conservative users.

Key Features:

  • Simple deposit/borrow interface
  • Four years of proven security and uptime
  • Liquid health factor indicators and alerts
  • Governance token (SLND) with voting rights

4. Save (Volt Strategy Vaults)

Save operates automated yield vaults that deploy deposited assets across multiple protocols to maximize returns. Instead of choosing which platform to use, you deposit into a "Volt" and Save manages the rest.

Available Volts:

  • USD++ Volt: Deposits USDC across Kamino, Marginfi, and Save itself, automatically rebalancing to the highest yield. Current APY: 9-12%.
  • SOL Yield Volt: Stakes your SOL natively, then uses the staked position to generate additional DeFi yield. Current APY: 8-11%.
  • Stablecoin Arbitrage Volt: Exploits price differences between USD-pegged assets across Solana DEXs. Current APY: 6-8%.

Yield Strategies for 2026

Conservative Strategy (6-9% APY)

Capital allocation: 100% USDC or USDT

Deposit stablecoins on Kamino Lend at 8-9% APY. No price volatility risk. Withdraw anytime. Ideal for capital preservation with inflation-beating returns.

Balanced Strategy (10-15% APY)

Capital allocation: 50% jitoSOL, 50% USDC

  1. Convert 50% of capital to jitoSOL (earns ~7.5% staking + 3.8% lending = ~11.3%)
  2. Deposit 50% as USDC on Kamino at ~8.5% APY
  3. Average portfolio yield: ~10%

Yield-Maximizing Strategy (15-20% APY)

Capital allocation: 100% jitoSOL

  1. Supply jitoSOL on Marginfi at ~3.5% + staking rewards
  2. Borrow USDC at 40-50% LTV (loan-to-value ratio)
  3. Deposit borrowed USDC on Kamino at ~8.5% APY
  4. Effective yield: ~11% (jitoSOL) + ~4% (leverage boost) = ~15%

⚠ Leverage Warning: Leveraged strategies can generate impressive returns, but they also introduce liquidation risk. If the value of your collateral drops (e.g., SOL price falls), you risk liquidation. Never exceed 50% LTV and monitor positions regularly.

How to Get Started on Kamino Lend

  1. Connect your wallet: Go to app.kamino.finance and connect Phantom or Solflare.
  2. Deposit: Choose an asset (USDC, SOL, jitoSOL) and click "Supply." Confirm the transaction.
  3. Start earning: Interest accrues in real-time. You can withdraw at any time (minus any outstanding borrows).
  4. Borrow (optional): Click "Borrow," choose an asset, enter amount, and confirm. Your collateral ratio must remain above the liquidation threshold.
  5. Monitor: Check your health factor regularly. A health factor below 1.0 triggers liquidation.

Risks of DeFi Lending

  • Smart contract risk: Lending protocols can have bugs. Kamino and Marginfi have been audited by multiple firms (Neodyme, OtterSec, Kudelski), but no audit guarantees perfect security.
  • Liquidation risk: If you borrow and your collateral value drops, you'll be liquidated. The protocol typically adds a 5-10% liquidation penalty.
  • Oracle risk: Price oracles can fail or be manipulated. Solana's Pyth and Switchboard oracles have proven reliable, but oracle failures have caused losses on other chains.
  • IL for leveraged positions: Yield-maximizing strategies using LSTs can suffer from impermanent loss if the LST/underlying asset ratio changes significantly.
