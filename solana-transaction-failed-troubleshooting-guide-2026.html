Solana Transaction Failed? Complete Troubleshooting Guide 2026

Published: June 1, 2026 | Updated: June 1, 2026 | 15 min read

You're not alone. Every day, thousands of Solana users see the dreaded "Transaction simulation: failed" or "Blockhash not found" error messages in Phantom, Solflare, or Jupiter. Your funds are still in your wallet, but the trade you wanted didn't go through—and while you're troubleshooting, the meme coin you were trying to buy just ran 40% without you.

Solana processes over 65 million transactions daily in 2026, and with that volume comes inevitable failures. But here's the truth: most failed Solana transactions are preventable. You just need to understand the four root causes and how to fix each one.

This guide covers every Solana transaction error you'll encounter in 2026—from priority fee miscalculations to slippage tolerance errors, blockhash expiration, and network congestion—with exact, actionable steps to fix each one.

💡 Quick Answer: Most failed Solana transactions are caused by one of four issues: (1) priority fees too low during congestion, (2) slippage tolerance too tight for volatile meme coins, (3) expired blockhash (transaction took too long to confirm), or (4) insufficient compute units for complex instructions. Fix #1 and #2 resolves 80%+ of failures.

Why Solana Transactions Fail: The 4 Root Causes

Before diving into fixes, you need to understand why Solana transactions fail. Unlike Ethereum where failures usually mean you lose gas fees, Solana only charges fees on successful transactions (with one exception we'll cover). This means you can retry for free, but it also means validators have less incentive to include marginal transactions during congestion.

1. Priority Fees Too Low — The #1 Cause of Failures

Solana uses a local fee market where each account has its own queue. When a popular token or DEX pair is congested, validators prioritize transactions that pay higher priority fees. If your priority fee is set to the minimum during a meme coin frenzy, your transaction may sit pending until the blockhash expires.

⚠️ Common Error: "Transaction simulation: failed. Reason: Transaction exceeded maximum compute budget."

2. Slippage Tolerance Too Tight

Meme coins on Solana are notoriously volatile. If you set 0.5% slippage and the price moves 3% between submission and confirmation, your transaction fails. This is Solana's safety mechanism protecting you from bad trades—but it also means you miss the trade entirely.

3. Blockhash Expired

Every Solana transaction includes a recent blockhash (proof the transaction was created recently). Blockhashes expire after ~150 blocks (about 60 seconds). If your wallet takes too long to sign, or the network is congested, the blockhash becomes stale and the transaction fails.

4. Insufficient Compute Units

Complex transactions—like Jupiter swaps involving multiple hops or token account creations—require more compute units than the default 200,000. When they exceed the budget, the transaction fails mid-execution.

How to Fix Failed Solana Transactions: Step-by-Step

1

Increase Your Priority Fee

In Phantom or Solflare, enable "Priority Fee" or "Network Fee" settings. During normal conditions, a priority fee of 0.00001 SOL (10,000 microlamports) is sufficient. During meme coin mania or NFT mints, increase to 0.001–0.01 SOL for faster confirmation.

Pro tip: Use Solscan's fee estimator to check current network congestion. Look for priorityFeeEstimate values—multiply the "medium" estimate by 1.5x for reliable inclusion.

2

Increase Slippage Tolerance

For volatile meme coins, increase slippage to 5–10%. Yes, this means you might get a slightly worse price, but it beats watching the trade fail entirely. Most DEX aggregators like Jupiter let you set slippage per-transaction.

Warning: Never set slippage above 15% on unknown tokens—this opens you to sandwich attacks and bad routing. Stick to 5–10% for established meme coins, and use "Auto Slippage" on Jupiter for safety.

3

Retry with a Fresh Blockhash

If you see "Blockhash not found" or "Transaction too large," simply retry the transaction. Most wallets auto-refresh the blockhash. If the error persists, close and reopen your wallet, or switch RPC endpoints in your wallet settings.

4

Increase Compute Unit Budget

For complex Jupiter swaps or token launches, manually set compute units to 400,000–600,000. In Phantom, go to Settings → Priority Fee → Advanced and adjust the compute unit limit. This costs slightly more in fees but ensures complex transactions succeed.

5

Switch RPC Endpoints

Your wallet connects to a specific RPC node. Some public RPCs are slower or more congested. Switch to a faster provider:

  • Helius (free tier available) — Fastest public RPC
  • Triton — Enterprise-grade RPC
  • GenesysGo — Reliable public endpoint
  • QuickNode — Premium RPC with low latency

In Phantom: Settings → Network → Custom RPC. Paste the endpoint URL and save.

6

Clear Your Wallet Pending Queue

If you have stuck pending transactions, they can block new ones. In Phantom, go to Settings → Clear Pending Transactions. On Solflare, use the "Cancel Transaction" option. You can also use Solscan to find and replace the stale transaction with a new one using the "durable nonce" method for advanced users.

Solana Transaction Pending: What to Do

A pending transaction is different from a failed one. Your transaction was submitted to the network but hasn't been confirmed yet. Here's what to do:

  1. Wait 60 seconds — Most pending transactions confirm or fail within one minute.
  2. Check Solscan — Paste your wallet address and look for the pending transaction under "Recent Transactions."
  3. Cancel or replace — Submit a new transaction with a higher priority fee and the same nonce (advanced). Most wallets now have a "Cancel" button for stuck transactions.
  4. Clear wallet cache — In Phantom: Settings → Troubleshooting → Clear Cache. This resets the local transaction state.

How Solana Transaction Fees Actually Work (2026 Edition)

If you want to understand the deeper mechanics, here's the technical breakdown:

  • Base fee: 5,000 lamports (0.000005 SOL) per signature. Fixed, non-negotiable.
  • Priority fee: Optional fee per compute unit (micro-lamports/CU). You set this amount. Higher = faster confirmation.
  • Compute units: Default 200,000 per transaction. Complex swaps may need 400,000–800,000.
  • Compute budget fee: Optional additional fee to increase your compute unit limit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I lose SOL if my transaction fails?

No. Solana only charges the base fee (5,000 lamports per signature) on successful transactions. If your transaction fails, you pay nothing. The one exception: if your transaction successfully simulates but fails during execution, you may still pay a small fee. This is rare and typically under 0.000005 SOL.

Why does my transaction fail on Jupiter but work on Raydium?

Jupiter aggregates multiple DEXs and may route through 3–4 hops. Each hop adds compute requirements. If the complex route fails, try disabling "Multi-hop" or switching to "Single-hop" mode in Jupiter's settings.

How do I fix "transaction simulation: failed" permanently?

The "transaction simulation: failed" error is Solana's way of saying your transaction would fail if submitted. The fix is almost always: increase priority fees (to 0.001–0.01 SOL), increase slippage (to 5–10%), and retry. If it still fails, check if the token you're buying has trading restrictions.

Can a failed transaction still take my SOL?

Extremely unlikely on Solana. The network validates transactions before execution. If simulation fails, no SOL leaves your wallet. However, always verify you're interacting with legitimate dApps—scam sites can trick you into approving malicious transactions. Read our Crypto Scam Prevention Guide for safety tips.

What is the best priority fee for Solana NFT mints?

For popular NFT mints in 2026, expect to pay 0.01–0.1 SOL in priority fees. Check the mint's Discord or X account for recommended fee levels before mint time. Many mints announce their suggested priority fees in the hours before launch.
