?? Crypto Staking Passive Income 2026: How to Earn 7-12% APY on Solana and Ethereum

Published: May 20, 2026 | Updated: May 20, 2026 | 11 min read

In 2026, crypto staking has become the closest thing to a crypto savings account except with 10x the yield. With Solana validators offering 7-8% APY, Ethereum at 3-4% through Lido, and liquid staking derivatives pushing 10-12% combined returns, staking is now the default strategy for anyone holding crypto longer than 30 days.

The beauty of staking? You earn rewards simply for holding tokens that help secure the network. No active trading. No timing the market. No complex DeFi strategies. Just consistent, compounding returns paid directly to your wallet.

?? The Numbers: A 10 SOL stake earning 7.5% APY grows to 10.75 SOL in one year that's 0.75 SOL (~$120 at Solana's $160 price) in passive income from a ~$1,600 investment. On a $10,000 portfolio spread across SOL, ETH, and liquid staking protocols, you could earn $800-$1,200 per year in staking rewards alone.

How Staking Works in 2026

At its core, staking is simple: you lock up your tokens to help validate transactions on a Proof-of-Stake blockchain, and you earn rewards proportional to your stake. But in 2026, the staking ecosystem has evolved far beyond simple validator delegation:

  • Native Staking: Delegate your SOL directly to a validator. Full control, no middleman, rewards paid in SOL.
  • Liquid Staking: Deposit tokens into a protocol (Marinade, Jito, Lido) and receive a liquid receipt token (mSOL, jitoSOL, stETH) that you can use in DeFi while earning staking rewards.
  • Restaking: EigenLayer and Jito Restaking let you "restake" your liquid staking tokens to secure additional protocols earning extra yield on top of your base staking rewards.
  • Auto-Compounding Vaults: Automated strategies that stake, claim rewards, and reinvest them without any manual intervention.

Solana Staking: The Best Yield for Everyday Investors

Solana's staking mechanism is one of the most accessible in crypto. With a minimum stake of just 0.01 SOL (~$1.60) and no lock-up period, anyone can start earning immediately. Here's how:

Step 1: Choose Your Staking Method

Step 2: Select a Validator

Not all validators are equal. When choosing a Solana validator through Phantom wallet, look for:

  • Uptime above 99.5% Validators with poor uptime miss blocks and reduce your rewards.
  • Commission below 10% The validator takes a cut of your rewards. Avoid validators charging 15%+.
  • No "superminority" concentration Avoid large validators that control too much stake (decentralization matters for long-term network health).
  • MEV-aware validators Jito validators redistribute MEV rewards to stakers, boosting yield by 0.3-0.5%.

Step 3: Monitor and Compound

Solana staking rewards are distributed every epoch (~2 days). You can claim them manually or let them accumulate. For maximum compounding, claim rewards every 2-4 weeks and restake them. On a 10 SOL stake, manual compounding adds approximately 0.3% to your effective APY.

? Pro Tip: Use Marinade Finance's auto-compounding vault if you don't want to manually claim and restake. It automatically claims your mSOL staking rewards and reinvests them into the staking pool, giving you compound interest without any active management.

Ethereum Staking: Lower Yield, Institutional-Grade Security

Ethereum's staking yield is lower (3-4% native) but offers deeper liquidity and more DeFi integrations. For long-term ETH holders, staking is essentially leaving yield on the table if you don't do it.

Advanced Strategies: Stacking Staking Yields

The real magic happens when you combine staking with DeFi a strategy called "yield stacking" that can push your effective returns to 10-15% APY:

Strategy 1: Liquid Stake + Lend

Stake SOL on Marinade to get mSOL (7.5% APY), then supply mSOL to Kamino Lend to earn an additional 2-4% in lending interest. Total return: 9.5-11.5% APY.

Strategy 2: Liquid Stake + LP

Stake ETH on Lido to get stETH (3.2% APY), then provide stETH/ETH liquidity on Curve or Balancer to earn an additional 3-8% in trading fees and protocol incentives. Total return: 6-11% APY.

Strategy 3: Restaking on EigenLayer

Restake your stETH or mSOL on EigenLayer (or Jito Restaking for Solana) to secure actively validated services (AVS), earning an additional 2-6% in restaking rewards. Total return: 9-14% APY.

?? Smart Contract Risk Warning: Every DeFi integration adds smart contract risk. A vulnerability in Marinade, Lido, or EigenLayer could result in partial or total loss of your staked assets. Never put more than 30% of your portfolio into a single yield-stacking strategy, and only use protocols that have been audited by at least two reputable firms.

Tax Implications of Staking Income in 2026

Staking rewards are treated as taxable income in most jurisdictions. In the US, the IRS treats staking rewards as ordinary income at the fair market value when received. Each claim event creates a taxable event. If you sell your staking rewards later, any price appreciation is taxed as capital gains.

Use a crypto tax calculator (like CoinTracker or Koinly) that tracks staking rewards automatically. Most Solana wallets now export staking reward history directly, making tax filing significantly easier than in previous years.

Common Staking Mistakes to Avoid

  • Staking on an exchange without researching their terms Some exchanges take 25-50% of your staking rewards as a fee. You're almost always better off staking natively or through a liquid staking protocol.
  • Not diversifying validators If you stake more than 10 SOL, split across 2-3 validators. If one goes offline or gets slashed, you only lose rewards on a portion of your stake.
  • Forgetting about unstaking times Native SOL staking takes 2-3 epochs (~4-6 days) to unbond. If you need to sell during a crash, you can't access those tokens immediately.
  • Ignoring MEV rewards Jito validators share MEV tips with stakers, boosting yield by 0.5-1%. Always check if your validator participates in MEV redistribution.

Final Thoughts: Make Your Crypto Work for You

If you're holding crypto and not staking, you're leaving guaranteed returns on the table. In 2026, with institutional-grade staking infrastructure, regulated liquid staking protocols, and compoundable yields, there's simply no reason to let your tokens sit idle.

Start small: stake 1 SOL on Marinade, try the mSOL experience, see how rewards accumulate. Once you're comfortable, scale up to a diversified staking portfolio that generates predictable, monthly passive income.
