Step 4: Set Stop-Losses on Your Accumulation (Yes, Even on Dips)

Buying the dip doesn't mean catching a falling knife without protection. If BTC breaks below $65K, the chart structure is broken and you need to reassess:

  • Portfolio-level stop: If total unrealized loss on accumulated positions exceeds 15%, pause all further buying and re-evaluate in 24 hours.
  • Token-level stop: Set a 20% trailing stop-below on Zone 1 buys once they're 5% in profit. This locks in gains without capping upside.
  • Time stop: If the market hasn't recovered within 72 hours, cut positions by 50% to preserve capital for the next opportunity.

Step 5: Have a Take-Profit Plan Before You Buy

The most common mistake in dip-buying is holding too long ” watching your 10% gain turn into a loss because you didn't have an exit. Know your targets before clicking "buy":

Target Return Action
T1 ” Initial Bounce +5% to +8% Sell 25% of accumulated position
T2 ” Recovery +12% to +15% Sell another 25%
T3 ” Rally Continuation +20% to +25% Sell 50% of remaining, let the rest ride
T4 ” New Highs +30%+ Hold remaining with a trailing stop

This structure means you take profit on 50% of your position during the bounce-and-recovery phase (T1+T2), and let the other 50% run for the big moves. If the bounce fails, you've already secured gains.

Three Common Dip-Buying Mistakes to Avoid

•Œ Mistake #1: Buying Without a Zone Structure

Going all-in at $67,777 means you're exposed if BTC drops to $66K or $65K. You're betting on a single price level instead of giving yourself room to average in. Always split your capital into at least 3 zones.

⚠ Mistake #2: Ignoring Funding Rates

During sharp selloffs, perpetual swap funding rates can go negative ” meaning shorts are paying longs. This is actually bullish for recovery. Check funding rates on Binance or Bybit before buying. If funding is deeply negative, the squeeze is coming.

•Œ Mistake #3: Buying Tokens Just Because They're Down the Most

A token down 30% might have further to fall. The ones down the most during a correction are often the weakest ” with low liquidity, fading narratives, or team selling. Focus on relative strength leaders like $DKING that are holding up despite the sell pressure.
