#1: Support/Resistance Scalping (Highest Win Rate)

This is the foundation strategy. Every meme coin on Solana trades within well-defined ranges during most market conditions. Your job is to identify these ranges and trade the boundaries.

1 Identify the Range

Use the 1-minute or 5-minute chart. Look for at least three touches at a support level and three rejections at a resistance level. The more touches, the stronger the level. On DexScreener or Birdeye, draw horizontal lines at these levels.

2 Wait for Confirmation

Don't buy the first touch. Wait for price to approach support, bounce slightly, and show a reversal candle (hammer, doji, or bullish engulfing on the 1-minute chart). Confirmation is everything.

3 Enter with Precision

Place your buy order at or just above the confirmed support bounce. Use limit orders to avoid paying the spread. On Jupiter, set a 0.5% slippage tolerance ” tight enough to protect against bad fills, loose enough to ensure execution.

4 Exit at Target

Sell at the identified resistance level. Take profit at 70-80% of the range to account for slippage and avoid getting rejected at the exact top. A 70% range capture on a $0.01-$0.02 range means $0.007-$0.014 profit per token.

5 Cut Losses Fast

If price breaks below support by more than 1%, exit immediately. No hesitation. A failed support level often drops 3-5% before finding the next level. Protect your capital.

#2: Momentum Scalping (Highest Reward)

Momentum scalping catches micro-trends within larger trends. When a meme coin starts moving on volume, early momentum scalpers ride the first wave and exit before the inevitable pullback.

The momentum scalping checklist:

  • Volume must be at least 2x the 5-minute average
  • Price must break above the previous 3 candles' highs
  • Buy orders on the order book must be accelerating (watch the depth chart)
  • Enter on the first pullback to the breakout level, not at the breakout itself
  • Take profit at 1.5x the initial breakout move
  • Stop loss at the breakout level minus 1%

Momentum scalping works best during high-volatility periods ” major BTC moves, listing announcements, or ecosystem news. During quiet consolidation, stick to support/resistance scalping instead.

#3: Mean Reversion Scalping (Lowest Risk)

Meme coins are famously volatile, but they also revert to the mean frequently. Mean reversion scalping exploits this by buying sharp dips and shorting sharp spikes, betting that price will return to its recent average.

When it works best:

  • After a 5-10% sudden drop with no fundamental catalyst (just market noise)
  • When RSI on the 1-minute chart drops below 20 (oversold)
  • When the order book shows support building at the dip level
  • During sideways markets where volatility is range-bound
Mean Reversion Setup: On Birdeye, set a 5-minute RSI indicator. When RSI drops below 25 and price is at a support level from the 15-minute chart, enter a scalp position targeting a return to the 5-minute EMA. Target: 60-80% of the dip retraced. Stop loss: below the dip by 1.5x the average candle range.

Mean reversion scalping has the highest win rate of any strategy ” often 70-80% ” but the smallest per-trade profits. It works because most sharp meme coin moves are noise, not signal. The key is strict discipline: if the dip keeps dipping past your stop loss, get out. Mean reversion fails catastrophically during trend days.

Risk Management: The Scalper's Survival Guide

Scalping without risk management is gambling. Period. Here are the non-negotiable rules every scalper must follow:

š¨ The #1 Scalping Killer: Overtrading. When you're tired, frustrated, or chasing losses, your edge disappears. Set a daily maximum of 20-30 trades and a daily loss limit of 5% of your capital. If you hit either limit, stop. Walk away. Tomorrow is another day.

The 1% Rule

Never risk more than 1% of your total trading capital on any single trade. If you have $1,000 in your scalping wallet, your maximum loss per trade is $10. This ensures that even a 10-trade losing streak only draws down 10% of your capital ” recoverable with a good session.

Position Sizing for Scalping

Your position size depends on your stop loss distance:

Position Size = (Account Risk %) / (Stop Loss %)

Example: 1% account risk, 2% stop loss = 50% position size. If your account is $1,000 and you use a 2% stop loss, your position size is $500.

The 3:1 Risk-Reward Fallacy

Traditional trading wisdom says you need a 3:1 risk-reward ratio. For scalping, this is wrong. Scalping works at 1:1 risk-reward with a 60%+ win rate. The math:

  • 60% win rate at 1:1 R:R = 20% return per 100 trades
  • 50% win rate at 1.5:1 R:R = 25% return per 100 trades
  • 70% win rate at 0.8:1 R:R = 26% return per 100 trades

The key is high win rate, not massive winners. Protect your capital, cut losses fast, and let small wins compound.

Scalping Psychology: The Hidden Skill

Here's the truth most scalping guides won't tell you: the hardest part of scalping isn't the strategy ” it's the psychology. Watching 20 trades in an hour, each one with real money on the line, triggers every emotional weakness you have.

FOMO: A token pumps 5% without you. You chase it. It dumps. You're caught at the top.

Greed: You're up 8% on the day. Your rules say stop. But you take "one more trade." You give back 3%.

Fear: A perfect setup appears. You hesitate. It runs without you. You spend the next hour trying to force bad trades.

Boredom: The market is slow. You take a low-probability trade just to feel alive. It loses.

The solution is not to eliminate these emotions ” that's impossible. The solution is to build a system so tight that your emotions don't matter. Pre-set stop losses. Daily limits written in stone. A trading journal that forces accountability. This is what separates professionals from gamblers.
