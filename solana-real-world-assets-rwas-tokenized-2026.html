10. Risks & Due Diligence

RWAs are not risk-free. Every category carries specific risks that differ from crypto-native DeFi:

Custodian Risk

The underlying asset is held by a third-party custodian. If the custodian becomes insolvent or commits fraud, the token may become worthless. Always verify the custodian's credentials â€” regulated entities with audited reserves (e.g., Anchorage, Coinbase Custody, BitGo) are preferable.

Smart Contract Risk

RWA protocols still rely on Solana smart contracts. A bug in the minting, redemption, or transfer logic could lead to loss of funds. Top protocols use multiple audits (Neodyme, Kudelski, Sec3) and real-time monitoring services.

Regulatory Risk

Many RWA tokens may be classified as securities. A regulatory crackdown could force protocols to freeze redemptions or delist tokens. The 2026 regulatory landscape is more favorable in the US (with the FIT21 framework providing clarity), but jurisdictions like the EU (MiCA) and Asia impose their own requirements.

Liquidity Risk

While tokenized Treasuries typically offer daily liquidity (1â€“5 day settlement), private credit pools and real estate tokens may have lockup periods of 30â€“90 days. In a market panic, redemption queues can form.

Oracle & Valuation Risk

Commodity and real estate tokens depend on price oracles. If the oracle feed is manipulated or goes stale, the token price may diverge from the underlying asset value. Protocols using Pyth Network (which dominates Solana oracle infrastructure) benefit from first-party data providers.

Due Diligence Checklist: Before investing in any Solana RWA product, verify: (1) Who is the custodian? (2) Are audits public? (3) What is the redemption timeline? (4) Is the pool KYC-gated or permissionless? (5) Has the team been transparent during past market stress events?
