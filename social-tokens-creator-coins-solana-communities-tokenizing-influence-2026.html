Creator Coins vs. Community Coins

Creator coins are issued by a specific individual â€" an influencer, artist, musician, or thought leader. The token's value tracks that individual's brand, career trajectory, and audience engagement. Community coins are issued by a collective rather than a single person. Evaluating Social Tokens: A Due Diligence Framework

Social tokens require a different analytical framework than traditional crypto assets. Here is a systematic approach:

1. Creator or Community Quality

  • Authenticity: Does the creator have a consistent track record? A creator with 3 years of output is worth more than one who went viral last month.
  • Engagement: Look beyond follower counts. What is the comment-to-view ratio? Discord activity levels? 50,000 engaged followers are worth more than 500,000 passive ones.
  • Revenue diversification: Creators with multiple income streams are less likely to abandon their token economy.

2. Tokenomics Design

3. Community Health & Liquidity

  • Is the community growing month-over-month? Use Solscan to track holder distribution.
  • Is the token on a reputable DEX (Jupiter, Raydium, Orca)?
  • What is daily trading volume? What is slippage on a $1,000 trade?
  • Is liquidity locked? Unlocked liquidity is a rug-pull risk.

Critical Warning: Never invest in a social token that lacks locked liquidity, has anonymous developers with no verifiable reputation, or promises guaranteed returns. Only invest where you can verify the creator's real-world identity.
