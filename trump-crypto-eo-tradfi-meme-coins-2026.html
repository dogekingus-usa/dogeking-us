Frequently Asked Questions

Does the EO mean crypto is now legal in the US?

Crypto was already legal in the US. The EO provides regulatory clarity about how crypto integrates with existing banking systems. It doesn't legalize crypto; it legitimizes it within the traditional financial system.

Will meme coins be classified as securities under the EO?

No. The EO's Digital Commodity classification explicitly covers most meme coins, removing them from SEC securities jurisdiction. This is one of the most favorable outcomes meme coin holders could have hoped for.

When will banks actually start offering crypto services?

The 90-day guidance deadline is May 2026. Most analysts expect the first bank crypto products to launch in Q3 2026, with broader adoption by Q1 2027.

How does this affect my $DKING holdings?

Positively. The EO removes regulatory barriers to exchange listings, bank custody, and institutional investment. As these channels open, $DKING gains accessibility to a much larger pool of capital.

Should I sell the news?

Historical patterns suggest that "sell the news" strategies have underperformed "accumulate through regulatory events" strategies by 3:1 over the past decade of crypto policy milestones. The EO's effects will unfold over months and years, not days.

Does this apply to all states?

Federal banking law preempts state law for nationally chartered banks. However, state-chartered banks and state-level crypto regulations may still vary. New York's BitLicense and California's digital asset laws still apply to state-chartered entities.

What happens to decentralized exchanges (DEXs) under the EO?

DEXs remain largely unaffected since they operate outside traditional banking channels. However, increased TradFi integration may actually drive more volume to DEXs as arbitrage opportunities between centralized and decentralized markets expand.

Will the next administration reverse this EO?

Executive orders can be reversed by subsequent presidents. However, the bipartisan nature of crypto legislation in 2026 and the fact that the EO builds on existing law (the Digital Asset Market Structure Bill) makes a full reversal unlikely regardless of election outcomes.
