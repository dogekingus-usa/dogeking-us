Why Rug Pulls Keep Happening

Rug pulls are the #1 cause of losses in meme coin trading. Not market crashes. Not bad entries. Not even bad projects — deliberate scams designed to take your money and disappear.

Here's how they work: A dev team launches a token, builds hype through paid influencers and fake community numbers, then drains the liquidity pool and disappears with millions. The entire cycle takes 48-72 hours.

By the time you hear about it, the liquidity is already gone.

The good news? Rug pulls follow patterns. They all share the same 7 red flags. Once you know what to look for, they're almost impossible to miss. Here's the exact checklist.

Red Flag #1: Locked or No Liquidity

🚩 Liquidity is NOT locked — or locked for less than 30 days

What to check: Use DEX Screener or RugCheck.xyz to verify if the liquidity pool (LP) is locked. A legitimate project locks LP for 6+ months or permanently. A rug project locks for 7 days — just long enough to build a market cap, then unlocks and dumps.

Green flag: LP locked for 1 year+ or via a trusted locker like Unicrypt or Team Finance.

Red flag score: ⚠️ HIGH — if LP is unlocked, do not buy.

Red Flag #2: Honeypot Tokenomics

🚩 You can buy but you can't sell

What to check: Does the token contract have a "transfer restriction" that only allows the dev wallet to sell? This is a honeypot — you buy in, the price goes up on your buy pressure, but when you try to sell, the transaction fails every time.

Green flag: No transfer restrictions. You can buy 1 token and sell it immediately in the same transaction to verify.

Red flag score: ⚠️ CRITICAL — honeypots are 100% loss.

Red Flag #3: High Tax / Slippage

🚩 Buy/sell tax over 10%

What to check: Every token has a transaction tax (buy tax, sell tax, or both). Legitimate projects set this at 3-6% to fund marketing and development. Rug projects set it at 15-20% — making it mathematically impossible to profit even if the price goes up.

Example: You buy $100 worth. 15% tax means only $85 reaches the pool. The price needs to go up 18% just to break even on your exit. The rug pulls before you get there.

Red flag score: ⚠️ HIGH — anything above 10% is designed to trap you.

Red Flag #4: Concentrated Dev Holdings

🚩 Dev wallet holds 20%+ of total supply

What to check: Use Solscan or Bubblemaps to check the top holders. If the deployer wallet or a cluster of connected wallets holds more than 20% of supply, they can dump on the market at any time.

The classic rug setup: Dev deploys with 100% supply. Distributes 20% to the liquidity pool. Keeps 80% across 5-10 wallets. Hype builds. Price pumps. Then all dev wallets sell at once. You're holding zero.

Red flag score: ⚠️ HIGH — check Bubblemaps for wallet clustering.

Red Flag #5: No Social Proof / Bot Army

🚩 Telegram has 10,000 members but only 5 people talking

What to check: Look at Telegram member quality. If the group has 10K+ members but engagement is under 1%, it's botted. Check Twitter: does the account have organic replies or just retweet bots and like-farming?

The giveaway: Scammers pay $50 for 10,000 bot members. One week of real marketing costs $5,000+. The bot army is cheaper and faster — and it's the #1 sign of a short-term scam.

Green flag: 500 real members with 20%+ engagement is worth more than 50,000 bots.

Red flag score: ⚠️ MEDIUM-HIGH — check engagement rate, not just member count.

Red Flag #6: Anonymous Dev With No Track Record

🚩 Dev is anonymous AND has no previous successful projects

What to check: Anonymity alone is not a rug flag — many legitimate meme coin devs are anonymous. But anonymity PLUS no track record PLUS no doxxed team members? That's the scam trifecta.

What to look for instead: A dev who has launched 3+ tokens that are still alive. A team that participates in community calls (even voice-only). A founder who shows up when problems arise.

Red flag score: ⚠️ MEDIUM — only dangerous when combined with other flags.

Red Flag #7: No Audit / No Renounced Contract

🚩 Contract is not renounced — dev can change the rules at any time

What to check: Is the contract ownership renounced? If not, the dev can mint new tokens, change fees, freeze wallets, or steal the entire pool. An unaudited, unrenounced contract is the single biggest red flag in crypto.

Green flag: Contract renounced to a dead address AND audited by a reputable firm (CertiK, Hacken, or at minimum a RugDoc review).

Red flag score: ⚠️ CRITICAL — if the contract is not renounced, assume scam until proven otherwise.

The Complete Pre-Buy Checklist

Before you buy any meme coin, check all 7:

  • ☐ LP locked 6+ months? (RugCheck.xyz)
  • ☐ No honeypot? (buy 1 token, try to sell it)
  • ☐ Tax under 10%? (DEX Screener)
  • ☐ Dev holds under 20%? (Bubblemaps)
  • ☐ Real community engagement > 5%? (Telegram/Twitter)
  • ☐ Dev has track record or doxxed team? (Previous projects)
  • ☐ Contract renounced + audited? (Solscan + audit report)

Score: 0-2 flags = caution. 3-4 flags = skip. 5-7 flags = confirmed scam.
