Solana vs Ethereum Gas Fees Comparison 2026

Gas fees are one of the most important factors when choosing a blockchain. Solana and Ethereum have fundamentally different fee structures. Here is how they compare in 2026.

Solana Fees

Solana transactions cost a fraction of a cent. A simple SOL transfer costs approximately $0.0002. A DeFi transaction costs $0.001-0.005. An NFT mint costs $0.002-0.01. Even during peak network usage, Solana fees rarely exceed $0.05. This makes Solana ideal for high-frequency trading, gaming, and microtransactions where Ethereum fees would be prohibitive.

Ethereum Layer 1 Fees

Ethereum L1 fees vary significantly based on network congestion. A simple ETH transfer: $0.50-3. A DeFi swap: $5-30. An NFT mint: $10-100+ during popular mints. Ethereum L1 fees make small transactions uneconomical. However, for large value transfers ($10,000+), the fee as a percentage is still reasonable.

Ethereum Layer 2 Fees

Ethereum L2 solutions have dramatically reduced fees. Arbitrum: $0.05-0.50 per transaction. Optimism: $0.05-0.40 per transaction. Base: $0.01-0.10 per transaction. L2 fees are approaching Solana-level affordability while maintaining Ethereum's security. The tradeoff is slightly slower transaction finality and additional wallet complexity.
