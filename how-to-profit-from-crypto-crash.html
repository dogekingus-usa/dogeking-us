Strategy 3: Shorting the Market (High Risk)

● Risk Level: High ● Difficulty: Advanced

Profit from further price declines using futures and options

If you believe the market has further to fall, shorting allows you to profit from the decline. But this is the most dangerous strategy — a sudden reversal can liquidate your position instantly. Only for experienced traders.

Safer shorting approach: Instead of opening leveraged shorts (which can get liquidated), use put options on exchanges that offer them. Your maximum loss is the premium paid, but your upside is unlimited if the market drops.

Alternatively: Use a hedging strategy — short the amount equal to your long position. If the market drops, your short profits offset your long losses. If the market rises, your long profits offset short losses.

Where to short: Bybit offers the best derivatives platform with tight spreads and advanced order types for experienced traders.

⚠️ Warning: Never short with leverage you can't afford to lose. A 10% upswing can liquidate a 10x leveraged short position.

Strategy 4: Arbitrage and Spread Trading (Medium Risk)

● Risk Level: Medium ● Difficulty: Advanced

Profit from price differences across exchanges and trading pairs

During crashes, exchanges process 10x normal volume and prices diverge significantly. The same coin can trade at different prices on different exchanges — sometimes by 2-5%.

Simple arbitrage: Buy on the exchange with the lowest price, transfer to the exchange with the highest price, sell. Requires speed, low fees, and multiple exchange accounts.

Spread trading: Set limit buy orders 3-5% below market and set limit sell orders 3-5% above market. In volatile conditions, both orders can fill within hours, giving you a 6-10% round trip profit.

Best setup: Having accounts on MEXC, Binance, and Bybit gives you full coverage for capitalizing on exchange price differences.
